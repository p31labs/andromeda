## OPTION A: Full Story (178 words)

My son Bash turns 10 on March 10th.

When he was younger, he asked why his brain worked differently. As an electrician who spent 16 years wiring nuclear submarines, I didn't reach for a metaphor — I drew him a circuit diagram.

It actually made sense to him.

Last year, at 40, I was diagnosed with AuDHD. Turns out, that wiring diagram was for me, too.

So, through my nonprofit P31 Labs, I built him something.

BONDING is a multiplayer chemistry game where you drag elements from the periodic table to build molecules. It teaches real science through play, and a dad can play right alongside his kid from a separate device.

I built it for Bash's 10th birthday. It's free. It's open source. Every molecule you build is a tiny act of creation.

Happy MAR10 Day, buddy.

After 16 years as an electrician on nuclear submarines, and recently founding P31 Labs to build open-source assistive tech, I wanted to create something that teaches real science through play.
