Work Control Document: WCD-SE02 - Spaceship Earth Architectural Synthesis
System Overview and Architectural Philosophy
The expansion of the progressive web application into the "Spaceship Earth" framework represents a paradigm shift in digital cohabitation and spatial computing. Designed to operate as a living Zoomable User Interface (ZUI) dashboard, the system is fundamentally engineered to mechanically resolve the "Double Empathy Problem"—the mutual breakdown in communication and understanding that occurs between differing neurotypes.1 By enforcing structural "Impedance Matching," the architecture functions as a psychological and communicative transformer, ensuring that intent is translated seamlessly across individual cognitive boundaries without signal degradation or interpersonal conflict.1
This Work Control Document provides a comprehensive Software Design Specification for the Spaceship Earth architecture. The system strictly adheres to a local-first, zero-trust infrastructure, ensuring absolute data sovereignty.2 By eliminating reliance on external cloud databases, the architecture mitigates systemic risks while ensuring that the core operating system truths remain cryptographically and structurally sound.2 The integration of behavioral psychology, specifically the Octalysis Framework, ensures that human motivation is optimized at every layer of the technology stack, bridging high-level psychological drives with low-level TypeScript and WebGL implementations.3 This document details the precise rendering pipelines, localized token economies, environmental sensor integrations, and edge-computed artificial intelligence required to operate the Citadel ecosystem.
Vector 1: The ZUI Mesh Visualizer
The ZUI Mesh Visualizer abandons the cognitively fatiguing "Infinite Scroll" paradigm in favor of intentional Z-axis navigation. Navigating the immense spatial complexity of the system's topology requires a physics engine that balances three-dimensional computing freedom with strict accessibility and cognitive load requirements, specifically targeting WCAG 2.2 AA compliance.2
To ground the user experience and prevent the disorientation commonly associated with unconstrained six-degrees-of-freedom environments, spatial navigation is governed by a dynamic Tri-State Camera paradigm.2 This paradigm allows the operator to seamlessly shift between interaction models based on their immediate cognitive needs. The Free Orbit Controls unlock the camera from any specific anchor, allowing the user to utilize standard three-dimensional rotation, panning, and zooming to freely explore decentralized molecular geometries and complex data clusters.2 When a user must focus on a centralized, highly structured data entity, the camera transitions into Dome Mode, which mechanically locks the zoom and orbit parameters into a fixed radius around a central target—such as an eighty-face observatory icosphere.2 This spatial constraint lowers the cognitive load required for navigation. Finally, for reviewing dense, flat analytical data within the spatial environment, Screen Mode physically locks the camera parallel with large, virtual two-dimensional terminal screens.2 This mode implements a haptic-enabled, infinite left-and-right scrolling interface that mimics the tactile resistance of a physical rotary encoder, providing necessary sensory grounding during extensive reading.2
Architectural Pattern for Zoom Levels (LOD)
The React Three Fiber architecture utilizes a strict Level of Detail (LOD) pattern to manage the transition between macro and micro spatial contexts. This is achieved using distance-based rendering components, specifically leveraging the @react-three/drei library's <Detailed> component to swap meshes and geometries dynamically based on camera proximity to the target coordinate.4 The Z-axis traversal is divided into three distinct ontological layers, each requiring highly specific rendering methodologies.
At the absolute macro scale resides Level 0, defined as the Sierpinski Tetrahedron Starfield or the Global Mesh. The visualizer renders a massive, decentralized fractal structure where the Sierpinski Tetrahedron serves as the geometric foundation.5 This recursive, self-similar geometry represents the interconnected, infinite potential of the global network.5 To render this efficiently, a custom GLSL shader material is applied to a heavily optimized InstancedMesh, treating the terminal vertices of the fractal not as solid objects, but as a vast, luminous starfield.7
As the camera breaches the Level 0 threshold and zooms inward, the fractal geometry undergoes a mathematically driven transformation, dissolving into Level 1, defined as the Local Zone Orbs. These orbs represent distinct physical or conceptual territories within the cohabitation ecosystem, such as a high-kinetic "Bash Zone" or a highly-ordered "Dad Zone". The architecture mandates a specific color-coding schema for these routing gateways, based directly on the elemental emission spectra of the P31 architecture.8

Page Buffering and View Frustum Culling on Memory-Constrained Devices
Exhaustive pre-launch testing of the three-dimensional visualizer revealed that maintaining sixty frames per second on low-tier, fifty-dollar Android devices and memory-constrained ESP32 client ecosystems caused critical WebGL Context Exhaustion.4 Violating the prime directive of universal accessibility is unacceptable within the Spaceship Earth paradigm. Therefore, the architecture enforces rigorous rendering optimizations, combining reactive frame looping with advanced spatial culling algorithms.
The continuous sixty-frames-per-second game-loop is inherently wasteful for a Zoomable User Interface where the user periodically stops to read, process information, or engage in deep focus. The architecture mandates the use of on-demand rendering by setting the <Canvas> property frameloop="demand".4 This explicitly decouples the render cycle from the hardware clock, tying the generation of a new frame directly to React state mutations or discrete camera control events. Whenever the camera position is updated via user input, the application invokes the invalidate function provided by the useThree hook, triggering a manual render frame only when strictly necessary.4
Furthermore, standard view frustum culling within the default rendering engine is highly inefficient for massive InstancedMesh deployments. The default engine calculates a single bounding sphere for the entire instance batch; if the camera intersects any fraction of this global bounding sphere, all instances are sent to the GPU, causing catastrophic overdraw and memory bloat on mobile clients.9 To resolve this bottleneck, the architecture integrates a specialized per-instance culling methodology utilizing spatial indexing algorithms.
The implementation relies on the integration of Bounding Volume Hierarchies and advanced instancing libraries, such as InstancedMesh2, which override the default draw call behaviors.10 The execution flow begins by offloading the mathematical computation of the Bounding Volume Hierarchy for all spatial coordinates of the Level 1 Orbs and Level 0 starfield nodes to a background Web Worker. During camera movement, the active view frustum is intersected against this Bounding Volume Hierarchy. The system then dynamically reorders the instanceMatrix array. It places all occluded or out-of-bounds instances at the end of the buffer and dynamically reduces the count property of the InstancedMesh, effectively hiding the instances without destroying their data.10 This operation guarantees that the GPU only processes vertex shaders for objects actively visible within the viewport.
Additionally, to prevent rapid memory accumulation, the application enforces strict lifecycle management of WebGL resources. Three.js and React Three Fiber do not automatically garbage-collect GPU resources.12 The architecture requires explicit invocation of the .dispose() method on all geometries, materials, and textures whenever a user transitions completely out of a Level of Detail bracket, ensuring the memory footprint remains perpetually stable.12
In scenarios where even highly optimized WebGL rendering fails to meet latency requirements, the architecture gracefully degrades to the "Jitterbug Navigator" pivot.8 This ultra-lightweight interface abandons heavy three-dimensional dependencies in favor of dynamically projected Scalable Vector Graphics driven by localized three-dimensional-to-two-dimensional orthographic projection mathematics.8 Operating as a psychophysiological state machine, the Jitterbug Navigator initiates in a "BREATHE" state, displaying a continuous 0.1 Hertz vagal breathing oscillation to induce cardiac coherence.8 Upon user interaction, it enters the "JITTERBUG" state, executing a biomimetic 1.5-second contraction that folds a twelve-vertex Cuboctahedron into a stable four-vertex tetrahedron.8 Finally, it settles into the "NAVIGATE" state, where the stable tetrahedron rotates at 0.5 revolutions per minute, providing a highly optimized, universally accessible routing core that executes in under one second on fragmented network connections.8
Vector 2: The Local-First Token Economy
The Spaceship Earth dashboard transcends traditional data visualization by operating as a gamified cohabitation engine. It relies on a complex, decoupled dual-currency ledger that translates psychological motivation into mechanical state. This system is engineered entirely upon local-first web technologies, utilizing Zustand for reactive user interface state management and PGLite—a full PostgreSQL instance compiled to WebAssembly—persisted via IndexedDB for immutable data sovereignty.13
Dual-Currency Mechanics and Octalysis Integration
The token economy is designed around two distinct vectors of human motivation, mapped directly to the eight core drives of the Octalysis Framework developed by behavioral designer Yu-kai Chou.3 By bridging high-level psychology with database mechanics, the system avoids superficial gamification in favor of deep, intrinsic motivation design.16
The first currency, "Spoons," is utilized for metabolic energy tracking. This mechanic maps directly to Octalysis Core Drive 6: Scarcity and Impatience, and Core Drive 8: Loss and Avoidance.3 Spoons represent finite physiological, emotional, and cognitive energy reserves. The system utilizes this depreciating currency to prevent thermal overload and burnout among users. As Spoons deplete through task execution or environmental stress, the interface reacts mechanically. Through the @p31labs/bus protocol, an instantaneous drop in the Spoon gauge broadcasts a signal that globally reduces the color saturation of the entire user interface within milliseconds.8 This visual desaturation serves as an unavoidable physiological cue, signaling the absolute necessity of rest and recovery.
The second currency, "Karma," functions as a peer-awarded reputation ledger for positive contributions to the cohabitation environment. This maps to Octalysis Core Drive 1: Epic Meaning and Calling, and Core Drive 5: Social Influence and Relatedness.3 Karma is a continuously accumulating metric that represents long-term territorial expansion and trust. Unlike Spoons, Karma cannot be spent or traded; it serves as an immutable, cumulative record of an individual's positive impact on the Spaceship Earth ecosystem.
Zustand Store Schema and PGLite Synchronization
Managing synchronous relational data without a centralized backend server represents a significant architectural challenge. The system achieves this through PGLite, running an embedded relational database entirely within the browser's execution environment, which mounts its data directory directly to the browser's native IndexedDB (idb://p31-ledger) for permanent, offline-capable storage.13 To bridge the reactive React frontend with the SQL ledger, a highly specific Zustand middleware pattern is employed.
Zustand acts as the volatile memory layer, providing instantaneous, synchronous user interface updates without blocking the main thread waiting for disk input/output operations. A custom Zustand persist middleware intercepts all state changes.17 Instead of simply dumping a JSON object into local storage, this middleware translates state mutations into asynchronous SQL INSERT and UPDATE statements directed at the PGLite worker.

It utilizes a dual-adapter strategy: a LocalStorageAdapter wraps all payloads in a strict p31: prefix (e.g., p31:spoons) to prevent namespace collisions, while a BroadcastAdapter utilizes the native BroadcastChannel API to facilitate direct, instantaneous, cross-tab message passing.8 This ensures that if a user spends Spoons in one tab, the gauge and color saturation drop synchronously in all other open windows without requiring a database read.
The "Help Board" and Formal Work Package Submission
The Help Board acts as the mechanical interface for resolving Octalysis Core Drive 2: Development and Accomplishment, and Core Drive 3: Empowerment of Creativity and Feedback.3 It facilitates the formal submission of Work Packages, which are tasks required for the maintenance or expansion of the physical or digital environment. The data flow for this system relies on strict transactional boundaries to maintain ledger integrity.
The flow initiates when a user generates a Work Package, such as proposing to reorganize a physical workspace. The client constructs a structured JSON object representing the task, calculating both the required Spoon expenditure to complete the task and the potential Karma yield upon successful peer review. Upon submission, the system triggers an atomic PostgreSQL transaction (db.transaction()) within PGLite.13 This transaction guarantees that the deduction of Spoons from the user's wallet and the creation of the pending Work Package record are cryptographically linked; if either operation fails, the entire state mutation is rolled back, preventing currency duplication or loss.
The task then enters a pending state on the Help Board. Once the task is physically completed, it requires cryptographic peer review. Another authorized user within the local offline network validates the completion of the work. Upon this cryptographic approval, a secondary SQL transaction executes, incrementing the worker's Karma balance.
Crucially, this peer-reviewed Karma accumulation is the sole mechanism for territorial expansion. If the user's total Karma crosses predefined mathematical thresholds, the system automatically triggers a schema update granting them "Creator Status" for new spatial zones.2 This grants the user write-access to unassigned Level 2 Context Faces within the ZUI. Because this expansion is permanently and immutably recorded in the local PostgreSQL database through verified peer transactions, it establishes a mathematically verifiable chain of trust and sovereignty that cannot be artificially manipulated by centralized administrators.
Vector 3: Environmental Nudging & The Visitor Mindset
To seamlessly bridge the physical environment with the digital interface, the Spaceship Earth architecture relies heavily on proximity-based environmental nudging. By detecting physical movements through space, the system forces a psychological event boundary reset, effectively transitioning the user's cognitive state to match the specific rules and expectations of the physical room they are entering.
Web Bluetooth API and Service Worker Constraints
The system utilizes Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE) beacons broadcasted by ESP32 microcontrollers deployed throughout the physical environment.21 These microcontrollers act as localized proxies, continuously advertising specific UUIDs associated with different spatial zones. However, architectural constraints inherent to modern Progressive Web Apps strictly limit how BLE scanning can be executed.
Modern browser security models prohibit Service Workers from performing continuous, passive background BLE scanning without explicit, persistent user interaction.23 The Web Bluetooth API cannot autonomously wake a suspended PWA solely based on the proximity of a new beacon due to strict battery optimization parameters (such as Android's Doze mode) and rigorous privacy guardrails designed to prevent unauthorized location tracking.26
To bypass these background limitations and achieve reliable environmental awareness, the architecture relies on a hybrid foreground-polling and visibility-state mechanism combined with local network relays.
First, upon opening the Progressive Web App, the user is prompted to initiate a foreground pairing session with the primary local ESP32 proxy, known as the "Citadel Hub." This is executed via the navigator.bluetooth.requestDevice() method, utilizing a filter array specifically targeting the custom GATT Service UUIDs assigned to the P31 architecture.28 The PWA maintains this active GATT connection while the application remains in the foreground.
Second, the architecture employs aggressive visibility state monitoring. A visibilitychange event listener is registered within the primary application thread.25 The exact moment the user unlocks their device or brings the Progressive Web App back into focus, the client immediately initiates a rapid polling sequence to the nearest ESP32 beacon to determine the current physical Zone.
Finally, as a robust fallback for when the Progressive Web App is fully suspended, the ESP32 hardware network tracks the user's wearable BLE tags independently. The ESP32 proxies push these threshold-crossing state changes to the local WiFi network via WebSockets. Because the device is connected to the local subnet, the PWA Service Worker can intercept these WebSocket broadcasts or utilize the local Push API to trigger an interface update the moment the application is resumed.24
The "Visitor Mindset" Onboarding Flow
When the hybrid detection system confirms that a user has crossed a physical threshold from one distinct spatial zone to another, it implements a strict psychological intervention termed the "Visitor Mindset." This concept is rooted in the psychology of territorial behavior and psychological ownership.31 When individuals enter a space governed by different cognitive rules or sensory expectations, interpersonal friction frequently occurs. The user interface flow mechanically induces an "Event Boundary"—a forced cognitive reset that requires the brain to segment the previous activity, discard previous working memory, and explicitly prepare for a new environmental context.
The user interface flow for the Visitor Mindset onboarding modal is highly orchestrated to maximize cognitive friction. The instant the BLE threshold crossing is confirmed by the local network, the Tri-State Camera forcefully transitions out of the immersive Free Orbit mode into a rigidly locked Screen Mode.2 Simultaneously, a full-viewport, frosted-glass modal overlay obscures the underlying ZUI dashboard. This aggressive visual occlusion prevents task continuation, forcing the user to halt their current digital activity and address the environmental change.
The modal explicitly displays the localized rules for the new zone. For instance, transitioning from the high-kinetic Bash Zone to the highly-ordered Dad Zone will render a prompt indicating the requirement for low-kinetic behavior, high-order categorization, and metabolic recovery. To dismiss the modal, the architecture mandates high-friction haptic acknowledgment. A simple tap interaction is programmatically rejected to prevent reflexive, muscle-memory dismissal. The user must perform a deliberate, sustained action—such as a continuous two-second swipe or dragging a heavily weighted digital slider across the screen. Only upon this deliberate acknowledgment does the user interface disengage the modal overlay, unlock the camera physics, and permit interaction with the local Zone Orb.
Vector 4: The Rules Engine & Cognitive Shielding
At the core of the Spaceship Earth cohabitation engine is the absolute requirement to mediate conflict before it escalates. The Double Empathy Problem dictates that individuals with varying cognitive profiles frequently misinterpret the intent, tone, and urgency of communications. The architecture solves this communication degradation through a rigorous legal ontology combined with on-device artificial intelligence mediation.
Data Model for the Constitution and Rules Engine
The system enforces a strict, three-tiered legal ontology, structured to allow both human readability and deterministic, algorithmic rule execution by the underlying software engine.32 This legal framework is serialized into the local IndexedDB store using a graph-based relational model that dictates system permissions and boundary constraints.

Client-Side "Cognitive Shield" LLM Integration
When interpersonal communication occurs within the dashboard—such as leaving a note on the Help Board or pinging a user in a different zone—high-voltage, emotionally charged text can trigger sensory overload or escalate conflict. The "Cognitive Shield" acts as an active, localized digital mediator. It intercepts raw, outgoing messages and utilizes a Large Language Model to algorithmically rewrite them into neutral, highly actionable formats before they are transmitted.35
To maintain absolute local sovereignty and ensure zero data leakage to corporate cloud providers, the language model must execute entirely on the client side or within the isolated local area network.37 The primary execution engine relies on WebAssembly ports of inference engines, such as WebLLM or Pyodide, allowing quantized, small-parameter models (e.g., Llama-3-8B-Instruct or specialized ShieldGemma variants designed for safety policy evaluation 39) to run directly within the browser's execution sandbox.35 If the mobile client lacks the necessary RAM to instantiate the WebAssembly model natively, the architecture implements a secure fallback. The request is routed over the encrypted local network to a dedicated Ollama endpoint running on the centralized, on-premise Citadel server hardware.37
The Cognitive Shield relies on rigorous prompt engineering to execute its mediation logic.43 The text is processed through a highly structured context window designed specifically for conflict resolution and emotional neutralization.
The algorithmic mediation flow executes in a strict sequence. First, the input is captured when a user types a highly emotional message into the interface. The system immediately intercepts this text and wraps it in a strict system prompt instruction: You are a Cognitive Shield agent. Your goal is to neutralize emotional volatility and extract the core operational request. Rewrite the following input using the BLUF (Bottom Line Up Front) framework. Remove all accusatory language. Output only the neutral summary and required action.
The language model processes the text locally and generates the BLUF output. For example, an input of "I am so sick of you leaving the Bash Zone a total mess, you never clean up your tools and it's driving me crazy!" is algorithmically transformed into "BLUF: The tools in the Bash Zone need to be put away. Action Required: Please organize the workspace."
Crucially, the system does not automatically send the altered message. The sender is presented with the neutralized text and must explicitly authorize the transmission of the BLUF summary. This authorization step forces a deliberate pause, introducing a secondary psychological Event Boundary that allows the sender's own emotional state to de-escalate. Once authorized, the neutralized text is appended to the local-first network and synchronized to the recipient. By algorithmically stripping the emotional volatility while preserving the actionable data, the Cognitive Shield completely bypasses the Double Empathy communication mismatch, ensuring that requests are received purely as operational requirements rather than personal attacks.
Conclusion and Operational Readiness
The Spaceship Earth architecture defined in WCD-SE02 establishes a highly resilient, offline-first operating environment capable of supporting complex spatial computing and social organization. By synthesizing the visual capabilities of React Three Fiber with aggressive view frustum culling and spatial indexing, the system guarantees smooth, WCAG 2.2 AA compliant accessibility even on severely memory-constrained hardware. Furthermore, the integration of PGLite and Zustand ensures that the sophisticated, Octalysis-driven dual-currency token economy functions flawlessly without reliance on vulnerable, centralized cloud infrastructure.
Ultimately, this architecture transcends standard software engineering by hardcoding psychological safety and behavioral alignment directly into its mechanical layers. Through the strict enforcement of the Visitor Mindset via Bluetooth Event Boundaries, and the deployment of a localized WebAssembly LLM Cognitive Shield, the platform creates a structural Impedance Match. This precise orchestration ensures that spatial sovereignty is respected, cognitive load is systematically minimized, and the Double Empathy Problem is mechanically neutralized, fulfilling the foundational mandate of the P31 Labs ecosystem.
