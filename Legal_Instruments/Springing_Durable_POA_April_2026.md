---

## 2. SPRINGING ACTIVATION CONDITIONS

Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 10-6B-9, this Power of Attorney is a "Springing" instrument. It shall NOT become effective immediately. It shall become effective ONLY upon the occurrence of one or more of the following conditions:

**A. Medical Emergency or Incapacity:** A licensed physician determines I am unable to manage my property or affairs due to physical or mental illness, specifically including but not limited to a hypocalcemic crisis resulting from my diagnosed Chronic Hypoparathyroidism.

**B. Detention or Incarceration:** I am detained, incarcerated, or otherwise involuntarily confined by any state, federal, or local law enforcement authority.

**C. Extreme Duress / ADA Title II Impairment:** My agent, Brenda O'Dell, submits a sworn affidavit stating that my diagnosed Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) and ADHD are causing severe executive dysfunction or a "fawn response," rendering me temporarily unable to safely process, review, or consent to binding legal documents under adversarial pressure.

---

## 3. GRANT OF GENERAL AND SPECIFIC AUTHORITY

Upon activation, my agent is authorized to act on my behalf in all lawful matters, with the following specific authorizations strictly emphasized:

**Claims and Litigation (O.C.G.A. § 10-6B-55):** My agent is explicitly authorized to assert and maintain before a court or administrative agency a claim, claim for relief, cause of action, counterclaim, offset, recoupment, or defense.

**Supported Decision-Making Lock:** My agent has the absolute, irrevocable authority to review, approve, reject, or modify any proposed settlement agreement, consent order, or binding contract in Johnson v. Johnson (Camden County Superior Court, Civil Action No. 2025CV936).

*No settlement or consent order shall be valid without my agent's explicit, 72-hour reviewed written approval if this document is active.*

---

## 4. DURABILITY PROVISION

This Power of Attorney shall not be affected by my subsequent disability, incapacity, or adjudged incompetency. It is a Durable Power of Attorney under the Georgia Uniform Power of Attorney Act.

---

## 5. RELIANCE BY THIRD PARTIES

Any person, including my agent, may rely upon the validity of this Power of Attorney or a copy of it unless that person knows it has terminated or is invalid.
