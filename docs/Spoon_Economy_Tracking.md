# Spoon Economy Tracking System

## Purpose
Monitor energy levels and resource needs during high-pressure legal and technical operations to maintain operational stability and prevent burnout.

## Core Metrics

### Current Spoon Count
**Scale:** 0-10 spoons
- **10:** Full energy, can handle complex tasks
- **7-8:** Good energy, productive work possible
- **5-6:** Moderate energy, routine tasks only
- **3-4:** Low energy, need rest or support
- **1-2:** Critical depletion, immediate rest required
- **0:** Burnout threshold - STOP ALL ACTIVITY

### Resource Inflows (Oxygen Support)
Track incoming support from community:
- **Financial Support:** Ko-fi donations, resource purchases
- **Technical Support:** Code reviews, validation, problem-solving
- **Emotional Support:** Encouragement, validation, stress relief
- **Practical Support:** Food, supplies, errands

### Energy Expenditure
Track energy spent on activities:
- **High Cost (2-3 spoons):** Complex coding, legal document review, strategic planning
- **Medium Cost (1-2 spoons):** Communication, coordination, routine tasks
- **Low Cost (0.5-1 spoon):** Passive monitoring, light reading
- **Restorative (-1-2 spoons):** Sleep, meals, breaks, support received

## Threshold Alerts

### Yellow Alert (3-4 spoons)
- **Action Required:** Take immediate break
- **Duration:** 15-30 minutes
- **Activities:** Hydration, light snack, brief walk
- **Support Needed:** Check for incoming Oxygen support

### Red Alert (1-2 spoons)
- **Action Required:** STOP current activity
- **Duration:** 30-60 minutes minimum
- **Activities:** Rest, meal, support engagement
- **Support Needed:** Request immediate Oxygen support

### Critical Alert (0 spoons)
- **Action Required:** Complete shutdown
- **Duration:** 2+ hours or until next day
- **Activities:** Sleep, medical attention if needed
- **Support Needed:** Emergency Oxygen support required

## Support Request Protocol

### When to Request Support
- Spoon count drops below 4
- Task requires more energy than available
- Emotional distress affects performance
- Technical challenges exceed current capacity

### How to Request Support
1. **Identify Need:** Be specific about what type of support is needed
2. **Use Community Channels:** Discord, Ko-fi, direct messages
3. **Leverage Element Roles:**
   - **Hydrogen:** Message amplification, moral support
   - **Carbon:** Technical validation, problem-solving
   - **Oxygen:** Resource provision, grounding support
   - **Phosphorus:** Core mission alignment, strategic guidance

### Support Response Expectations
- **Immediate (0-15 min):** Acknowledgment and basic support
- **Short-term (15 min - 2 hours):** Resource provision, technical help
- **Long-term (2+ hours):** Sustained support, strategic assistance

- Share spoon count status with trusted community members

- Use Element roles to distribute support responsibilities

## Emergency Protocol

### Burnout Prevention
1. **Recognize Early Signs:** Irritability, confusion, physical symptoms
2. **Immediate Action:** Stop all non-essential activities
3. **Seek Support:** Contact emergency support network
4. **Recovery Plan:** Minimum 24 hours complete rest

### Support Network Activation
- **Primary Contacts:** Trusted community members with Oxygen role
- **Secondary Contacts:** Technical support (Carbon role) for task coverage
- **Emergency Contacts:** Medical or professional help if needed

## Success Metrics

### Individual Stability
- Maintain spoon count above 4 during critical periods
- Complete essential tasks without burnout
- Receive adequate Oxygen support when needed

### Community Effectiveness
- Support requests met within expected timeframes
- Balanced distribution of support across Element roles
- Positive feedback from support providers

### Mission Continuity
- Critical tasks completed despite resource constraints
- No emergency shutdowns due to preventable burnout
- Sustainable pace maintained throughout operation
