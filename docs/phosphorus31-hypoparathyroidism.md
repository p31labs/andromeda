# Hypoparathyroidism: The Neurological Reality of an Endocrine Disorder

Hypoparathyroidism is a rare endocrine disorder characterized by absent or abnormally low parathyroid hormone (PTH), causing severe disruptions in calcium and phosphorus homeostasis. While traditionally treated as a purely metabolic issue, the cognitive and neuropsychiatric effects are chronically underrepresented in clinical practice.

## The Lived Experience (2003–Present)

Living with chronic hypoparathyroidism means managing a calcium regulation disorder without adequate support infrastructure or monitoring technology equivalent to a diabetes CGM. In my case, this began with a seizure at age 18 in 2003, leading to a non-surgical etiology. For over 22 years, the reality has been that **stress is the mechanism of harm** — cortisol directly suppresses PTH, triggering a calcium crisis.

**My numbers (January 2026):**
- Serum calcium: **7.8 mg/dL** (normal range: 8.5–10.5 mg/dL)
- PTH: **<6 pg/mL** (normal range: 15–65 pg/mL — functionally undetectable)

Like diabetes but way worse. Same endocrine failure category. Zero monitoring technology. Zero public understanding.

## The Cognitive Endpoint: What the Data Shows

The medical community is only beginning to document what patients have known for decades. The neurological impact is measurable and significant:

**Attention & Processing Speed:** 56.7% of chronic HypoPT patients show quantifiable deficits (JCEM 2025, Tmava-Berisha et al., PMC12261092).

**Executive Dysfunction:** 60.0% present with severe executive dysfunction — which directly overlaps with AuDHD presentations.

**Brain Structure:** MRI imaging shows reduced hippocampal volume that correlates with patient-reported "brain fog."

**Neurological vs. Psychiatric Symptoms:** HypoPT causes real neurological symptoms (tetany, paresthesia, seizures, cognitive slowing) that can easily be misread as a psychiatric presentation.

## The Diagnostic Danger: Misattributing Neurology to Psychiatry

When acute stressors — such as court proceedings, custody disputes, or eviction threats — elevate cortisol, it directly suppresses PTH. This directly triggers the calcium dysregulation that produces acute neurological symptoms.

Without proper medical context, these symptoms can be dangerously misattributed to psychiatric conditions. When a system creates extreme stress, it triggers the underlying condition. If it then observes the resulting high-volume, intense output and labels it "manic," it is observing the physiological harm it is causing and misattributing it to a psychiatric episode.

**If the output remains coherent, technically verified, and peer-reviewed, it is not mania. It is hyperfocus operating under extreme metabolic duress.**

## The Yorvipath Access Crisis

In August 2024, the FDA approved Yorvipath (palopegteriparatide), the first prodrug of PTH(1-34). In clinical trials, 95% of patients achieved independence from conventional calcium/vitamin D therapy. Ascendis Pharma reported FY2025 Yorvipath revenue of €477M with 5,300+ U.S. patients enrolled.

While it represents a functional treatment that can free patients from conventional therapy, it remains inaccessible to many due to its profound cost — **$150,000 to $285,000 per year.** For patients without income or adequate insurance coverage, a prescribed, life-altering medication remains completely out of reach.
