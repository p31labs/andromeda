P31 Labs builds open-source assistive technology for neurodivergent individuals — educational games, cognitive prosthetics, and communication tools designed for the brains that use them.

### Description (long)
P31 Labs, Inc. is a Georgia domestic nonprofit corporation (EIN: 42-1888158) dedicated to developing open-source assistive technology for neurodivergent individuals.

Our flagship product BONDING is a molecular chemistry game designed for neurodivergent families — kids build water molecules, parents breathe alongside them from separate devices, and every interaction is a documented act of care. It runs 413 automated tests and ships free at bonding.p31ca.org.

We also develop Node Zero, an ESP32-based cognitive prosthetic with haptic feedback and LoRa mesh networking; The Buffer, a communication processor that detects people-pleasing language patterns; and P31 Mesh, a WebRTC peer-to-peer breathing sync tool.

Our research series — The Tetrahedron Protocol — spans 17 papers covering quantum cognition, network topology, care economics, and cognitive engineering, published under ORCID 0009-0002-2492-9079.

We believe the best assistive technology is built by the people who need it.
