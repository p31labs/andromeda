## 1. ZERO-KNOWLEDGE IDENTITY & HIPAA SAFE HARBOR

P31 Labs takes the privacy of neurodivergent users and users with chronic health conditions as absolute. The System does not collect, process, or store Protected Health Information (PHI) as defined by the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA).

### 1.1 The Cryptographic Fingerprint

The System implements a "Zero-Knowledge" identity architecture.

- **No Personal Identifiers:** We do not collect names, dates of birth, Social Security Numbers, or medical diagnoses.
- **Anonymous Identity:** User identity is abstracted into a one-way, irreversible cryptographic hash (SHA-256).
- **Spoons Tracking:** All systemic tracking of cognitive energy ("Spoons" or "Karma") is tied strictly to this anonymous cryptographic hash.

### 1.2 HIPAA Safe Harbor Compliance

Because the data stored in Upstash Redis and IPFS cannot be reverse-engineered to identify an individual without their private cryptographic key, the data satisfies the HIPAA Safe Harbor De-identification Standard (45 CFR §164.514(b)).

**Safe Harbor Requirements Met:**
- All 18 HIPAA identifiers are removed
- No reasonable basis exists to believe the information can be used to identify an individual
- Cryptographic hash provides mathematical guarantee of de-identification

## 3. CRYPTOGRAPHIC ANONYMITY GUARANTEES

### 3.1 SHA-256 Hash Security

The System utilizes SHA-256 cryptographic hashing to ensure complete user anonymity:

- **One-Way Function:** SHA-256 is mathematically irreversible
- **Collision Resistance:** No two inputs can produce the same hash
- **Pre-image Resistance:** Cannot determine input from hash output
- **Avalanche Effect:** Small changes in input produce drastically different hashes

### 3.2 Zero-Knowledge Proof Architecture

The System implements zero-knowledge principles:

- **No Identity Storage:** P31 Labs never stores or has access to user identities
- **User-Controlled Keys:** Only users possess the ability to link their hash to their identity
- **Decentralized Storage:** No central database exists that could be compromised

### 3.3 Medical Device Data Protection

As a Class I medical device under FDA 21 CFR §890.3710, P31 Labs implements additional data protection measures:

- **Medical Device Standards:** Compliant with medical device data security requirements
- **Risk Management:** ISO 14971 risk management principles applied to data privacy
- **Quality Management:** ISO 13485 quality management system for data handling
