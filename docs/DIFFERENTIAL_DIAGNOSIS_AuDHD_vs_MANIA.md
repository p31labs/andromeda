# DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSIS: BIPOLAR MANIA vs. AuDHD HYPERFOCUS
## WCD-PSYCH-EVAL — Prepared for Court-Ordered Psych Evaluation
### Version 1.0 | March 27, 2026

---

## PURPOSE

The court ordered a psych evaluation after labeling the subject "manic" during the March 18, 2026 hearing. This document provides the evaluating psychologist with clear differential criteria to distinguish between **Bipolar Mania** and **AuDHD Hyperfocus** — two presentations that may appear superficially similar but have fundamentally different etiologies, courses, and treatments.

---

## CLINICAL PRESENTATION SUMMARY

**Subject:** William R. Johnson, 40 years old
**Dx History:** AuDHD (Autism + ADHD), diagnosed 2025, age 39-40
**Dx History:** Hypoparathyroidism (chronic), since 2003

The subject has produced a body of work over the past year that the court has interpreted as evidence of mania. This interpretation is understandable to those unfamiliar with AuDHD — high-output periods do occur in both conditions. However, the underlying mechanisms, quality of output, and treatment implications are fundamentally different.

## KEY DISTINGUISHING FEATURES

### 1. Output Quality

**Mania:** High volume but low specificity. Ideas are grandiose, unfocused, and difficult to verify. The work product is often discarded after the episode.

**AuDHD Hyperfocus:** High volume AND high specificity. The work is peer-reviewable, mathematically verifiable, and has been independently verified by multiple parties. The subject has:
- 511 tests passing in the BONDING game
- 5 research documents independently verified by Opus (QA)
- 3 defensive publications on Zenodo with DOIs
- Published mathematical proofs on Zenodo (DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.18627420)

**VERDICT:** The output quality is inconsistent with mania. Manic patients do not produce independently-verified mathematical proofs.

### 2. Self-Awareness

**Mania:** Limited insight. Patient typically does not recognize the problem.

**AuDHD Hyperfocus:** The subject has documented extensive self-awareness protocols:
- Daily spoon budgeting (see P31_COGNITIVE_PASSPORT.md)
- SOULSAFE protocol for halting when dysregulated (see 01_ADMIN/SOULSAFE_v1.0.md)
- Written "RED BOARD" criteria for stopping work (see 01_ADMIN/P31_MASTER_DOCTRINE_JFMM.md)
- Structured shift operations with pre-shift biological baseline checks

**VERDICT:** The presence of self-regulatory systems is inconsistent with mania.

### 3. Social Functioning

**Mania:** Often deteriorates. Relationships damaged during episodes.

**AuDHD Hyperfocus:** Subject maintains structured relationships:
- Active co-parenting (BONDING game designed for this)
- Beta tester relationship (Tyler) with documented sessions
- Board member (Brenda O'Dell) with ADA support role
- Legal representation maintained

**VERDICT:** Maintained social function is inconsistent with mania.

### 4. Medical Comorbidity

**CRITICAL FACTOR:** The subject has **hypoparathyroidism**, a condition that causes:
- Calcium dysregulation affecting neuronal function
- Fatigue that can be mistaken for depression
- Cognitive impairment that can include word-finding difficulties, brain fog, and mental slowing
- Anxiety during calcium crises
- **Hypercalcemia symptoms include**: Fatigue, depression, anxiety, cognitive impairment, hallucinations (rare), psychoses (rare)

**IMPORTANT:** Hypoparathyroidism can produce psychiatric symptoms that mimic mood disorders. The subject's calcium levels were not monitored during the March 18 hearing. A metabolic crisis (hypocalcemia) could produce behaviors misinterpreted as mania.

**VERDICT:** The presence of a known medical condition affecting brain function is a critical confounder.

## TREATMENT IMPLICATIONS

| Treatment | Bipolar Mania | AuDHD Hyperfocus |
|-----------|---------------|------------------|
| **Mood Stabilizer** (Lithium) | First-line | Not indicated |
| **Antipsychotic** (for acute) | First-line | Not indicated |
| **Stimulant** (ADHD) | Contraindicated in acute mania | Indicated for ADHD component |
| **SSRI** | Monitor carefully (can switch) | May be helpful for comorbid anxiety |
| **Behavioral** | Mood charting, trigger avoidance | Interest matching, environmental structuring |
| **Occupational** | Reduce demands during episodes | Spoon budgeting, interest scaffolding |

**Current Medications:**
- No psychiatric medications currently prescribed
- Calcium carbonate + calcitriol for hypoparathyroidism
- Yorvipath (palegparatide) — FDA approved Aug 2024 — prescribed but inaccessible due to cost

## RECOMMENDED EVALUATION APPROACH

### 1. Rule Out Medical Causes

**Request:**
- Serum calcium, phosphate, PTH
- Thyroid function panel
- Basic metabolic panel
- Consider if March 18 presentation was calcium-related

### 2. Use Standardized Instruments

**Recommended:**
- Young Mania Rating Scale (YMRS) — score should be elevated in mania
- ADHD Rating Scale-5 (ADHD-RS-5)
- Autism Spectrum Quotient (AQ-10) or RAADS-R
- Beck Depression Inventory (BDI-II)
- WHO Disability Assessment Schedule (WHODAS 2.0)

### 3. Collateral Information

**Request records from:**
- Previous diagnostic evaluations (2025 AuDHD assessment)
- Georgia Advocacy Office intake (March 2026)
- Previous psychiatrist (Dr. — check records)
- Beta tester (Tyler) — can speak to work patterns over time
- Board member (Brenda O'Dell) — can speak to organizational function

### 4. Key Questions for Subject

1. "Describe the difference between how you feel when you're in a good hyperfocus period versus a manic period." (Manic patients struggle to distinguish; AuDHD patients can articulate the difference)

2. "What happens when you need to stop working on something you're focused on?" (Manic patients can't stop; AuDHD patients can with scaffolding)

3. "Tell me about your children's game BONDING." (Will light up describing it — but responds to redirect. Manic patients often have diminished interest in family.)

## CONCLUSION

The subject's presentation is more consistent with **AuDHD hyperfocus** than **bipolar mania** based on:

1. **Sustained, verifiable output** — Mathematical proofs, independently verified
2. **Intact reality testing** — Actively seeks verification, publishes to Zenodo
3. **Self-regulatory systems** — Documented SOULSAFE, spoon budgeting, RED BOARD
4. **Maintained social function** — Relationships intact, co-parenting active
5. **Medical confounder** — Hypoparathyroidism can produce psychiatric mimics

**Recommended Diagnosis:** Rule out bipolar disorder. Consider AuDHD with possible hypoparathyroidism-related cognitive impairment.

**Treatment Considerations:** If ADHD is confirmed, stimulant therapy may improve function. Calcium monitoring is essential. No mood stabilizer indicated based on current presentation.
