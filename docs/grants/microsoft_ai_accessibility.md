### 1. The Problem: The Serialization Bottleneck & Decoherence

Standard assistive technology assumes a hardware deficit (e.g., screen readers for vision loss). It fails neurodivergent users (AuDHD) because the deficit is not in data acquisition, but in data serialization and social routing.

* **The Serialization Bottleneck:** The AuDHD internal state is high-fidelity and geometrically connected. Translating this into linear verbal speech acts as a low-bandwidth channel, causing massive packet loss (Transducer Error).

* **Decoherence (The Fawn Response):** Under social or adversarial pressure (e.g., family court proceedings), the neurodivergent operator's authentic internal state collapses to match external expectations.

### 2. The Solution: AI as a Cognitive Prosthetic

P31 Labs is building a suite of open-source tools that use AI not as a generic chatbot, but as an integrated cognitive prosthetic—a "Centaur" model (Human + AI > either alone).

**Key AI-Driven Innovations:**

1. **Fawn Guard:** A software buffer that intercepts and processes communication, utilizing LLMs to detect people-pleasing patterns and psychological decoherence before the user transmits a compromised response.

2. **The Triad of Cognition (Tag-Out System):** A specialized routing protocol that tags in specific AI agents (Sonnet, Gemini, DeepSeek, Opus) for specific operational domains (Mechanic, Narrator, Firmware, Architect) to bypass executive dysfunction and maintain system coherence.

3. **BONDING Telemetry:** An active multiplayer application providing court-admissible, cryptographically signed logs of parental engagement.
