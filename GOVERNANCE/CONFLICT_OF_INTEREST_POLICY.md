# P31 Labs Conflict of Interest Policy

**Effective Date:** March 24, 2026  
**Version:** 1.0.0  
**Organization:** P31 Labs, Inc. (Georgia 501(c)(3) Nonprofit)

## Policy Statement

P31 Labs is committed to maintaining the highest standards of ethical conduct and ensuring that all decisions are made in the best interests of the organization and its mission. This Conflict of Interest Policy establishes procedures to identify, disclose, and manage actual or potential conflicts of interest involving directors, officers, key employees, and significant contractors.

## Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to:
1. Protect the Corporation's interests when it is contemplating entering into a transaction or arrangement that might benefit the private interests of an officer or director or entities in which these individuals have a material financial interest
2. Ensure that the Corporation's decisions are made objectively and without improper influence
3. Maintain public trust and confidence in the Corporation's integrity
4. Comply with IRS requirements for 501(c)(3) organizations

## Scope

This policy applies to:
- All members of the Board of Directors
- All officers of the Corporation
- Key employees (those with decision-making authority over $10,000)
- Significant contractors (those receiving $5,000 or more annually)
- Immediate family members of the above individuals

## Definitions

**Conflict of Interest:** A situation where an individual's private interests could improperly influence the performance of their official duties and responsibilities to the Corporation.

**Interested Person:** Any director, principal officer, or member of a committee with governing board delegated powers who has a direct or indirect financial interest, as defined below, in any transaction, arrangement, or relationship or a series of similar transactions, arrangements, or relationships with the Corporation.

**Financial Interest:** A direct or indirect economic or financial interest in any entity with which the Corporation is negotiating or has a relationship, including but not limited to:
- Ownership of 5% or more of the voting stock or equity in an entity
- Employment by an entity (other than the Corporation)
- Receipt of compensation, fees, or benefits from an entity
- Service as an officer, director, trustee, partner, or consultant to an entity
- Family relationships with individuals who have financial interests in entities doing business with the Corporation

## Disclosure Requirements

### Annual Disclosure
All covered individuals must complete and submit a Conflict of Interest Disclosure Form annually, disclosing:
1. **Financial Interests:** All financial interests in entities that do business with or could potentially do business with the Corporation
2. **Outside Employment:** All employment, consulting arrangements, or business relationships
3. **Family Relationships:** Financial interests of immediate family members (spouse, domestic partner, parents, children, siblings)
4. **Gifts and Entertainment:** Any gifts, entertainment, or favors exceeding $100 from entities doing business with the Corporation

### Ongoing Disclosure
Covered individuals must disclose any new potential conflicts within 10 days of becoming aware of them.

### Disclosure Form
The Conflict of Interest Disclosure Form shall include:
- Full description of the financial interest
- Nature and extent of the relationship
- Potential impact on Corporation decisions
- Steps taken to avoid conflict

## Prohibited Transactions

The Corporation shall not engage in any transaction or arrangement where:
1. A director, officer, or their family has a direct or indirect financial interest
2. The transaction is not in the best interest of the Corporation
3. Proper disclosure and approval procedures are not followed
4. The transaction would constitute an excess benefit transaction under IRS rules

## Approval Procedures

### Step 1: Disclosure
The interested person must:
- Disclose the conflict in writing to the Board Chair and Secretary
- Provide full details of the financial interest and relationship
- Recuse themselves from initial Board discussion

### Step 2: Review
The Board must:
- Review the disclosure in executive session (without the interested person present)
- Determine if a conflict exists
- Assess the materiality and significance of the conflict
- Consider alternatives to the proposed transaction

### Step 3: Approval
For transactions with conflicts to be approved:
1. The interested person must recuse themselves from voting
2. Approval requires a majority vote of disinterested directors
3. The transaction must be fair and reasonable to the Corporation
4. The transaction must be in the Corporation's best interests
5. The transaction must not result in private inurement or excess benefit

### Step 4: Documentation
All conflict of interest transactions must be documented with:
- Written disclosure from the interested person
- Board resolution approving the transaction
- Minutes of the executive session discussion
- Justification for the transaction

## Special Provisions for Medical Device Development

### Technology Development Conflicts
Given the Corporation's focus on medical device development, special attention must be paid to:
1. **Personal Goodwill Protection:** Any technology developed by founders must be clearly documented as personal property to preserve asset protection strategies
2. **Licensing Agreements:** Personal technology licensed to the Corporation requires full disclosure and independent valuation
3. **Consulting Arrangements:** Medical and technical consultants must disclose all potential conflicts
4. **Research Partnerships:** Academic and industry partnerships require careful review for conflicts

### IP Assignment Requirements
All intellectual property assignments must:
- Include clear documentation of personal vs. organizational ownership
- Preserve the "Functional Goodwill" argument for asset protection
- Comply with Miller v. Miller precedent requirements
- Include appropriate licensing terms for continued personal use

## Enforcement and Penalties

### Violations
Violations of this policy include:
- Failure to disclose a conflict of interest
- Participation in decisions where a conflict exists
- Approval of prohibited transactions
- Failure to recuse oneself from voting

### Penalties
Violations may result in:
- Written warning
- Suspension of Board or committee membership
- Removal from Board or committee
- Legal action for damages
- Reporting to appropriate authorities (IRS, Attorney General)

### Whistleblower Protection
The Corporation shall protect individuals who report potential conflicts in good faith from retaliation.

## Medical Device Compliance Conflicts

### Regulatory Relationships
Board members and officers must disclose:
- Relationships with FDA officials or consultants
- Financial interests in regulatory consulting firms
- Past employment with medical device companies
- Participation in industry trade associations

### Clinical Trial Conflicts
For medical device clinical trials:
- Investigators must disclose all financial relationships
- Independent review committees must be free of conflicts
- Patient recruitment must not involve improper inducements
- Data analysis must be conducted without bias

## Annual Review and Training

### Policy Review
The Board shall review this policy annually and recommend updates as needed.

### Training Requirements
All covered individuals must complete annual training on:
- Conflict of interest identification
- Disclosure procedures
- Medical device compliance requirements
- Ethical decision-making

### Certification
All covered individuals must annually certify that they have:
- Read and understood this policy
- Completed required training
- Disclosed all potential conflicts
- Will comply with all policy requirements

## Records Retention

### Documentation Requirements
The Corporation shall maintain records of:
- All conflict of interest disclosures
- Board approvals of conflicted transactions
- Meeting minutes documenting conflict discussions
- Training completion records
- Violation reports and resolutions

### Retention Period
All conflict of interest records shall be retained for 7 years after the date of the transaction or the end of the individual's service, whichever is later.

## Excess Benefit Transactions

### Prohibited Acts
The Corporation shall not enter into any "excess benefit transaction" as defined by IRC Section 4958, which includes any transaction in which an economic benefit is provided to, or for the use of, a disqualified person and the economic benefit provided to the disqualified person exceeds the value of the consideration received for providing the benefit.

### Disqualified Persons
Disqualified persons include:
- Substantial contributors and their family members
- Corporation managers and their family members
- 35% controlled entities

### Correction Procedures
If an excess benefit transaction is discovered:
1. The transaction must be corrected immediately
2. The disqualified person must return the excess benefit
3. The Corporation must take steps to prevent recurrence
4. The transaction may be subject to IRS excise taxes

## Implementation

### Effective Date
This policy is effective March 24, 2026.

### Initial Compliance
All current directors, officers, and key employees must complete conflict of interest disclosures within 30 days of this policy's adoption.

### Policy Distribution
This policy shall be distributed to all covered individuals and made available to the public upon request.
