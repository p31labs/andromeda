# P31 Labs Bylaws

**Effective Date:** March 24, 2026  
**Version:** 1.0.0  
**Organization:** P31 Labs, Inc. (Georgia 501(c)(3) Nonprofit)

## Article I: Organization

### Section 1.1: Name
The name of this organization shall be **P31 Labs, Inc.**, hereinafter referred to as "the Corporation."

### Section 1.2: Purpose
The Corporation is organized exclusively for charitable, educational, and scientific purposes within the meaning of Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986, as amended, or the corresponding provision of any future United States Internal Revenue Law.

**Primary Mission:**
To create technology that amplifies human potential, fosters genuine community connections, and builds a more equitable and accessible digital future through the development of assistive technologies, open-source software, and educational initiatives.

**Specific Objectives:**
1. Develop and distribute assistive communication technologies for neurodivergent individuals
2. Create open-source tools that promote digital sovereignty and accessibility
3. Provide educational resources and training in ethical technology development
4. Advocate for neurodiversity rights and digital accessibility standards
5. Conduct research in quantum-ready systems and post-quantum cryptography

### Section 1.3: Nonprofit Status
The Corporation shall not engage in any activities not permitted of organizations exempt from federal income tax under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code.

### Section 1.4: Medical Device Compliance
The Corporation recognizes that certain technologies developed (specifically the Phenix Navigator system) may be classified as Class II medical devices under 21 CFR § 890.3710. All development, testing, and distribution activities shall comply with applicable FDA regulations while maintaining the Corporation's nonprofit status and mission.

## Article II: Members

### Section 2.1: Classes of Members
The Corporation shall have one class of members: **Contributing Members**.

### Section 2.2: Eligibility
Any individual who:
- Subscribes to the Corporation's mission and values
- Contributes time, resources, or expertise to the Corporation's objectives
- Agrees to abide by these Bylaws and the Corporation's policies
- Is approved by the Board of Directors

### Section 2.3: Rights and Privileges
Contributing Members shall have the right to:
- Attend and participate in annual meetings
- Vote on matters requiring member approval
- Receive regular reports on Corporation activities
- Access member-only resources and communications
- Run for election to the Board of Directors

### Section 2.4: Termination of Membership
Membership may be terminated for:
- Violation of Corporation policies or bylaws
- Conduct detrimental to the Corporation's mission
- Non-participation for 24 consecutive months
- Voluntary resignation

## Article III: Board of Directors

### Section 3.1: Powers
The Board of Directors shall manage the affairs of the Corporation and exercise all powers conferred by law and these Bylaws.

### Section 3.2: Number and Qualifications
The Board shall consist of 5-9 members who:
- Demonstrate commitment to the Corporation's mission
- Possess relevant expertise in technology, healthcare, law, or nonprofit management
- Have no conflicts of interest with the Corporation's objectives
- Are not compensated for Board service (except for reasonable expenses)

### Section 3.3: Election and Term
- Directors shall be elected by the Members at the annual meeting
- Terms shall be 3 years, with staggered elections
- No director may serve more than 3 consecutive terms
- Initial board members may be appointed by the incorporators

### Section 3.4: Removal and Resignation
- Directors may be removed for cause by a two-thirds vote of the remaining directors
- Resignations must be in writing and delivered to the Secretary
- Vacancies shall be filled by Board appointment until the next annual meeting

### Section 3.5: Meetings
- Regular meetings: Quarterly via video conference
- Special meetings: As called by the Chair or upon request of 3 directors
- Quorum: Majority of directors
- Action without meeting: Permitted with unanimous written consent

### Section 3.6: Committees
The Board may establish standing and ad hoc committees including:
- Executive Committee (3 members)
- Finance Committee
- Medical Device Compliance Committee
- Technology Ethics Committee
- Governance Committee

## Article IV: Officers

### Section 4.1: Officers
The officers shall be:
- Chair
- Vice Chair
- Secretary
- Treasurer
- Executive Director (non-voting, ex-officio)

### Section 4.2: Election and Term
- Officers elected annually by the Board from among its members
- Term: 1 year, renewable once consecutively
- No individual may hold more than one office simultaneously

### Section 4.3: Duties

**Chair:**
- Preside at all meetings of the Board and Members
- Coordinate Board activities and committee work
- Represent the Corporation externally
- Ensure implementation of Board decisions

**Vice Chair:**
- Assist the Chair in all duties
- Assume Chair responsibilities in absence
- Lead strategic planning initiatives

**Secretary:**
- Maintain corporate records and bylaws
- Record and distribute meeting minutes
- Manage official correspondence
- Maintain member records

**Treasurer:**
- Oversee financial management and reporting
- Present financial statements to the Board
- Ensure compliance with financial policies
- Coordinate annual audits

**Executive Director:**
- Implement Board policies and decisions
- Manage day-to-day operations
- Supervise staff and contractors
- Execute contracts and agreements

### Section 4.4: Removal
Any officer may be removed by a two-thirds vote of the Board for:
- Neglect of duties
- Conduct prejudicial to the Corporation
- Violation of Corporation policies

## Article V: Medical Device Compliance

### Section 5.1: Regulatory Oversight
The Board shall establish a Medical Device Compliance Committee responsible for:
- Ensuring FDA compliance for Class II devices
- Overseeing quality management systems
- Managing risk assessments and mitigation
- Coordinating with regulatory consultants
- Maintaining documentation for 21 CFR Part 820 compliance

### Section 5.2: Quality Management System
The Corporation shall implement and maintain a Quality Management System (QMS) that includes:
- Design controls per 21 CFR Part 820.30
- Risk management per ISO 14971
- Document control procedures
- Training programs for staff and contractors
- Supplier qualification and management

### Section 5.3: Device Classification Protocol
All technology development shall follow this protocol:
1. **Initial Assessment:** Determine if device meets FDA definition
2. **Classification Review:** Apply 21 CFR § 890.3710 criteria
3. **Exemption Analysis:** Evaluate 510(k) exemption eligibility
4. **Documentation:** Maintain technical files and design history
5. **Compliance Monitoring:** Ongoing regulatory surveillance

### Section 5.4: Personal Goodwill Protection
The Corporation shall implement procedures to:
- Maintain clear separation between personal and organizational IP
- Document the non-transferable nature of assistive technologies
- Preserve the "Functional Goodwill" argument for asset protection
- Ensure compliance with Miller v. Miller precedent

## Article VI: Conflicts of Interest

### Section 6.1: Policy Statement
The Corporation is committed to avoiding conflicts of interest and maintaining the highest standards of ethical conduct.

### Section 6.2: Disclosure Requirements
All directors, officers, and key personnel must annually disclose:
- Financial interests in organizations doing business with the Corporation
- Relationships that could influence decision-making
- Outside employment or consulting arrangements
- Intellectual property interests related to Corporation activities

### Section 6.3: Prohibited Transactions
The Corporation shall not engage in transactions where:
- A director, officer, or their family has a direct or indirect financial interest
- The transaction is not in the best interest of the Corporation
- Proper disclosure and approval procedures are not followed

### Section 6.4: Approval Process
Transactions with potential conflicts require:
- Full disclosure to the Board
- Recusal of interested parties from discussion and voting
- Approval by disinterested directors
- Documentation in meeting minutes

## Article VII: Financial Management

### Section 7.1: Fiscal Year
The fiscal year shall begin on January 1 and end on December 31.

### Section 7.2: Budget
- Annual budget prepared by Executive Director and Treasurer
- Budget approved by Board before fiscal year begins
- Quarterly financial reports to Board
- Annual financial statements audited by independent CPA

### Section 7.3: Contracts and Agreements
- Contracts over $10,000 require Board approval
- Medical device development agreements require dual approval (Board + Medical Device Compliance Committee)
- All contracts must include appropriate indemnification and liability clauses
- IP assignment clauses must preserve personal goodwill protections

### Section 7.4: Banking
- Bank accounts in the Corporation's name only
- Signature authority: Any two of: Chair, Treasurer, Executive Director
- Regular reconciliation and review procedures
- Segregation of duties for financial transactions

## Article VIII: Indemnification

### Section 8.1: Director and Officer Protection
The Corporation shall indemnify directors and officers to the fullest extent permitted by Georgia law against all expenses, judgments, fines, and amounts paid in settlement incurred in connection with any proceeding arising from their service.

### Section 8.2: Insurance
The Corporation may purchase and maintain insurance on behalf of any person serving as director or officer against any liability asserted against them.

### Section 8.3: Medical Device Liability
The Corporation shall maintain appropriate professional liability insurance covering:
- Medical device development and distribution
- Professional errors and omissions
- Product liability for assistive technologies
- Directors and officers liability

## Article IX: Amendments

### Section 9.1: Bylaw Amendments
These bylaws may be amended by:
- Two-thirds vote of the Board of Directors
- Majority vote of Members at annual or special meeting
- Written consent of two-thirds of Members

### Section 9.2: Notice Requirements
Proposed amendments must be:
- Submitted in writing to the Secretary
- Distributed to Board members at least 10 days before meeting
- Distributed to Members at least 30 days before member vote

### Section 9.3: Restrictions
No amendment shall:
- Be inconsistent with the Corporation's 501(c)(3) status
- Violate Georgia nonprofit corporation law
- Diminish the rights of existing members
- Alter the Corporation's fundamental purpose without IRS approval

## Article X: Dissolution

### Section 10.1: Voluntary Dissolution
The Corporation may be dissolved by:
- Two-thirds vote of the Board of Directors
- Majority vote of Members
- Judicial proceeding

### Section 10.2: Asset Distribution
Upon dissolution, assets shall be distributed to one or more organizations with similar purposes, as determined by the Board, which are exempt under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code.

### Section 10.3: Medical Device Assets
In the event of dissolution:
- Medical device designs and IP shall be transferred to organizations continuing similar work
- Patient data and records shall be handled per HIPAA requirements
- Regulatory approvals and clearances shall be transferred as appropriate

## Article XI: Parliamentary Authority

### Section 11.1: Rules of Order
The rules contained in the current edition of Robert's Rules of Order Newly Revised shall govern the Corporation in all cases not specifically provided for in these bylaws.
