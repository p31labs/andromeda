# P31 Labs Whistleblower Policy

**Effective Date:** March 24, 2026  
**Version:** 1.0.0  
**Organization:** P31 Labs, Inc. (Georgia 501(c)(3) Nonprofit)

## Policy Statement

P31 Labs is committed to maintaining the highest standards of ethical conduct, legal compliance, and financial integrity. This Whistleblower Policy provides a mechanism for employees, volunteers, contractors, and other stakeholders to report suspected violations of law, regulations, or organizational policies without fear of retaliation.

## Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to:
1. Encourage the prompt reporting of suspected misconduct or violations
2. Provide multiple channels for reporting concerns confidentially and anonymously
3. Ensure that all reports are investigated promptly and thoroughly
4. Protect individuals from retaliation for good faith reports
5. Maintain compliance with federal and state whistleblower protection laws
6. Preserve the integrity of the organization's medical device development programs

## Scope

This policy applies to:
- All employees (full-time, part-time, temporary)
- Board members and officers
- Volunteers and interns
- Independent contractors and consultants
- Third-party vendors and suppliers
- Anyone conducting business with or on behalf of P31 Labs

## Protected Disclosures

Individuals are protected when reporting in good faith any suspected:
- Violations of federal, state, or local laws
- Violations of organizational policies or procedures
- Financial improprieties or fraud
- Misuse of organizational resources
- Conflicts of interest
- Violations of medical device regulations (FDA, 21 CFR)
- Patient safety concerns
- HIPAA violations
- Discrimination or harassment
- Retaliation against other whistleblowers
- Any other activity that may harm the organization or its mission

## Reporting Channels

### Internal Reporting
Reports may be made through the following channels:
1. **Direct Supervisor or Manager**
2. **Executive Director**
3. **Board Chair**
4. **Designated Compliance Officer**
5. **Anonymous Hotline** (to be established)
6. **Written Report** to: compliance@p31labs.org

### External Reporting
Individuals may also report concerns to external authorities, including:
- **IRS Form 13909** (for 501(c)(3) violations)
- **Georgia Attorney General's Office**
- **FDA Office of Criminal Investigations** (for medical device violations)
- **Office of Inspector General** (for healthcare fraud)
- **Equal Employment Opportunity Commission** (for discrimination)

## Reporting Procedure

### Making a Report
Reports should include:
- Description of the concern or violation
- Date(s) and location of the incident(s)
- Names of individuals involved (if known)
- Names of witnesses (if any)
- Any supporting documentation

### Anonymous Reports
Anonymous reports are accepted and will be investigated to the extent possible. However, providing contact information allows for follow-up questions and updates on the investigation status.

### Good Faith Requirement
This policy protects individuals who make reports in good faith. Reports made with malicious intent or known to be false are not protected and may result in disciplinary action.

## Investigation Process

### Initial Assessment
1. **Receipt:** All reports will be acknowledged within 3 business days
2. **Assessment:** The Executive Director or designated investigator will assess the report's credibility and scope
3. **Assignment:** Appropriate personnel will be assigned to investigate based on the nature of the allegation

### Investigation Standards
Investigations will be:
- **Prompt:** Initiated within 5 business days of report receipt
- **Thorough:** Include interviews, document review, and evidence collection
- **Impartial:** Conducted by individuals without conflicts of interest
- **Confidential:** Limited to those with a need to know

### Investigation Timeline
- **Simple matters:** Resolution within 15 business days
- **Complex matters:** Resolution within 30 business days
- **Extenuating circumstances:** May require additional time with notification to reporter

### Investigation Documentation
All investigations will be documented, including:
- Report details and initial assessment
- Investigation plan and procedures
- Interview notes and evidence collected
- Findings and conclusions
- Recommended actions
- Follow-up measures

## Protection Against Retaliation

### Prohibited Retaliation
P31 Labs strictly prohibits retaliation against individuals who:
- Report suspected violations in good faith
- Participate in investigations
- Oppose practices they reasonably believe to be unlawful
- Exercise their rights under this policy

### Examples of Retaliation
Retaliation includes but is not limited to:
- Termination, demotion, or suspension
- Harassment, intimidation, or threats
- Unfair work assignments or schedule changes
- Negative performance evaluations
- Exclusion from meetings or communications
- Spreading false rumors or gossip

### Retaliation Reporting
Any suspected retaliation should be reported immediately using the same channels as original reports. Retaliation will be investigated promptly and may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.

## Medical Device Compliance Protections

### FDA Whistleblower Protections
Employees involved in medical device development are protected under:
- **21 U.S.C. § 307** (FDA Whistleblower Protection)
- **Occupational Safety and Health Act** (OSHA)
- **Sarbanes-Oxley Act** (for publicly traded entities)

### Protected Medical Device Disclosures
Reports related to medical device compliance are protected when they involve:
- Adulteration or misbranding of devices
- Violations of FDA regulations
- Failure to comply with quality system requirements
- Falsification of device records or data
- Obstruction of FDA investigations

### FDA Reporting Process
For medical device violations, reports may be made to:
- **FDA MedWatch Program** (1-800-FDA-1088)
- **FDA Office of Criminal Investigations** (1-866-300-4374)
- **FDA Division of Drug Information** (1-855-543-3784)

## Confidentiality

### Confidentiality Commitment
P31 Labs will maintain the confidentiality of reports and investigations to the extent possible while conducting a thorough investigation.

### Limitations on Confidentiality
Confidentiality may be limited when:
- Disclosure is necessary for investigation purposes
- Required by law or court order
- Needed to protect the safety of individuals
- Required for disciplinary action

### Reporter Confidentiality
The identity of reporters will be protected except when:
- The reporter consents to disclosure
- Disclosure is necessary for investigation
- Required by law
- Needed to implement corrective action

## Disciplinary Actions

### Violations of This Policy
Violations of this policy, including retaliation, will result in disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment or contracts.

### False Reports
Reports made in bad faith or with malicious intent may result in disciplinary action against the reporter.

### Investigation Findings
Based on investigation findings, appropriate action may include:
- No action (if allegation is unfounded)
- Corrective action (if violation is confirmed)
- Disciplinary action (for policy violations)
- Training or policy updates
- Referral to law enforcement (for criminal activity)

## Training and Communication

### Annual Training
All employees and board members must complete annual training on:
- This Whistleblower Policy
- How to report concerns
- Protection against retaliation
- Medical device compliance requirements

### Policy Communication
This policy will be:
- Distributed to all employees and board members
- Posted in employee areas
- Included in employee handbooks
- Made available to the public upon request

## Records Retention

### Documentation Requirements
The Corporation shall maintain records of:
- All reports received
- Investigation procedures and findings
- Disciplinary actions taken
- Training completion records
- Policy acknowledgments

### Retention Period
All whistleblower records shall be retained for 7 years after the resolution of the matter or the end of the individual's relationship with the Corporation, whichever is later.

## Legal Protections

### Federal Protections
Individuals are protected under various federal laws, including:
- **Sarbanes-Oxley Act** (for financial reporting)
- **Dodd-Frank Act** (for securities violations)
- **False Claims Act** (for fraud against the government)
- **Occupational Safety and Health Act** (for workplace safety)
- **FDA Whistleblower Protection** (for medical device violations)

### State Protections
Georgia law provides additional protections for whistleblowers, including:
- **Georgia Whistleblower Statute** (O.C.G.A. § 45-1-4)
- **Georgia False Medicaid Claims Act**
- **Georgia Workers' Compensation Retaliation Protections**

### Legal Assistance
Individuals who believe they have been retaliated against may seek legal assistance and file complaints with:
- **Equal Employment Opportunity Commission (EEOC)**
- **Georgia Commission on Equal Opportunity**
- **Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA)**
- **Department of Labor**

## Policy Review and Updates

### Annual Review
This policy shall be reviewed annually by the Board of Directors and updated as necessary to ensure compliance with changing laws and regulations.

### Policy Amendments
Amendments to this policy require approval by the Board of Directors and shall be communicated to all affected individuals.

### Effectiveness Monitoring
The Corporation shall monitor the effectiveness of this policy through:
- Tracking report volumes and types
- Measuring investigation timeliness
- Assessing reporter satisfaction
- Reviewing retaliation incidents
- Evaluating policy compliance
