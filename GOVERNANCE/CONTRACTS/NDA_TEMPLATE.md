## 1. DEFINITIONS

### 1.1 "Confidential Information" means any non-public information, knowledge, or data of the Disclosing Party, whether oral, written, electronic, or in any other form, including but not limited to:

**Medical Device Information:**
- Design specifications for assistive communication devices
- Technical drawings, schematics, and engineering data
- Software code, algorithms, and firmware
- Manufacturing processes and quality control procedures
- Regulatory submissions and FDA correspondence
- Clinical trial data and patient information
- Hardware Security Module (HSM) implementations
- Biometric authentication systems

**Business Information:**
- Business plans, strategies, and financial information
- Research and development activities
- Intellectual property filings and patent applications
- Customer lists and supplier information
- Marketing plans and pricing strategies
- Partnership agreements and fiscal sponsorship arrangements

**Technical Information:**
- Source code, object code, and software architecture
- Database designs and data structures
- Network configurations and security protocols
- API specifications and integration methods
- Encryption algorithms and cryptographic implementations
- Post-quantum cryptography implementations

### 1.2 "Purpose" means the evaluation and potential collaboration regarding [SPECIFIC PURPOSE].

### 1.3 "Personal Goodwill" means the non-transferable intellectual property and technological developments that are inextricably bound to the identity of the Disclosing Party's founder(s) and qualify as separate property under Georgia law (Miller v. Miller precedent).

## 2. OBLIGATIONS OF RECEIVING PARTY

### 2.1 Confidentiality
The Receiving Party shall:
- Keep all Confidential Information strictly confidential
- Not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without prior written consent
- Use the same degree of care to protect Confidential Information as it uses for its own confidential information (but no less than reasonable care)
- Limit disclosure to employees, contractors, or agents who need to know and are bound by written confidentiality obligations

### 2.2 Use Restrictions
The Receiving Party shall use Confidential Information solely for the Purpose and not for any competitive or commercial purpose.

### 2.3 Personal Goodwill Protection
The Receiving Party acknowledges and agrees that certain Confidential Information constitutes Personal Goodwill that:
- Is non-transferable and inseparable from the Disclosing Party's founder(s)
- Qualifies as separate property under Georgia family law
- Is protected from division in bankruptcy proceedings
- Cannot be licensed, assigned, or transferred without explicit written consent

## 3. EXCLUSIONS

Confidential Information does not include information that:
- Is or becomes publicly known through no fault of the Receiving Party
- Was rightfully known to the Receiving Party prior to disclosure
- Is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of Confidential Information
- Is rightfully received from a third party without restriction
- Is required to be disclosed by law, court order, or government authority (with prior notice to Disclosing Party)

## 4. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY RIGHTS

### 4.1 Ownership
All Confidential Information remains the exclusive property of the Disclosing Party. This Agreement does not grant any license or rights to the Receiving Party except as expressly stated.

### 4.2 Personal Goodwill Rights
The Receiving Party expressly acknowledges that:
- Personal Goodwill belongs exclusively to the Disclosing Party's founder(s)
- No rights to Personal Goodwill are granted under this Agreement
- Personal Goodwill cannot be assigned, licensed, or transferred
- Personal Goodwill is protected from creditor claims and bankruptcy proceedings

### 4.3 Improvements and Derivatives
Any improvements, modifications, or derivative works based on Confidential Information shall be the exclusive property of the Disclosing Party.

## 5. TERM AND TERMINATION

### 5.1 Term
This Agreement shall remain in effect for [NUMBER] years from the effective date, except for trade secrets, which shall remain protected indefinitely until they cease to qualify as trade secrets.

### 5.2 Return of Information
Upon termination or at the Disclosing Party's request, the Receiving Party shall:
- Return all Confidential Information in any form
- Destroy all copies, notes, or summaries
- Provide written certification of compliance
- Retain only one copy for legal compliance purposes

### 6.2 Quality Management
The Receiving Party shall not modify, reverse engineer, or otherwise alter Confidential Information in a manner that could affect:

- Risk management documentation
