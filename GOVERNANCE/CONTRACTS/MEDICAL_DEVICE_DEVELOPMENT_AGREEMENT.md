# MEDICAL DEVICE DEVELOPMENT AGREEMENT

**This Agreement is made effective as of:** [DATE]  
**Between:** P31 Labs, Inc., a Georgia nonprofit corporation ("Sponsor")  
**And:** [DEVELOPER NAME], an individual/entity ("Developer")

## RECITALS

WHEREAS, the Sponsor is engaged in the development of assistive communication technologies for neurodivergent individuals; and

WHEREAS, the Developer possesses expertise in medical device development and regulatory compliance; and

WHEREAS, the parties wish to collaborate on the development of medical device technology subject to FDA regulation;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants contained herein, the parties agree as follows:

## 1. DEFINITIONS

### 1.1 "Medical Device" means any device intended for use in the diagnosis, cure, mitigation, treatment, or prevention of disease in humans, as defined by 21 U.S.C. § 321(h), including but not limited to assistive communication devices.

### 1.2 "Development Work" means all activities performed by the Developer under this Agreement, including but not limited to:
- Design and development of medical device hardware and software
- Risk assessment and management
- Quality system implementation
- Regulatory submission preparation
- Clinical evaluation and testing

### 1.3 "Regulatory Requirements" means all applicable laws, regulations, and guidance documents, including but not limited to:
- 21 CFR Part 820 (Quality System Regulation)
- 21 CFR § 890.3710 (Powered Communication Systems)
- 21 CFR Part 812 (Investigational Device Exemptions)
- ISO 14971 (Risk Management)
- IEC 62304 (Medical Device Software)

### 1.4 "Personal Goodwill" means the non-transferable intellectual property and technological developments that are inextricably bound to the identity of the Sponsor's founder(s) and qualify as separate property under Georgia law.

## 2. SCOPE OF WORK

### 2.1 Development Services
The Developer shall perform the Development Work as specified in **Exhibit A** attached hereto and incorporated by reference.

### 2.2 Quality System Compliance
The Developer shall:
- Implement and maintain a quality management system compliant with 21 CFR Part 820
- Follow design control procedures per 21 CFR Part 820.30
- Maintain design history files and technical documentation
- Conduct risk assessments per ISO 14971

### 2.3 Regulatory Compliance
The Developer shall:
- Ensure compliance with all applicable Regulatory Requirements
- Prepare and submit necessary regulatory documentation
- Cooperate with FDA inspections and investigations
- Maintain records for regulatory review

### 2.4 Personal Goodwill Protection
The Developer acknowledges and agrees that:
- Certain technology constitutes Personal Goodwill
- Personal Goodwill remains the exclusive property of the Sponsor's founder(s)
- Development work must preserve asset protection strategies
- No rights to Personal Goodwill are granted to the Developer

## 3. DEVELOPER OBLIGATIONS

### 3.1 Professional Standards
The Developer shall perform all Development Work:
- In a professional and workmanlike manner
- In accordance with industry standards for medical device development
- Using qualified personnel with appropriate training and experience
- In compliance with all applicable laws and regulations

### 3.2 Documentation Requirements
The Developer shall maintain complete and accurate records of all Development Work, including:
- Design and development plans
- Risk management files
- Design verification and validation records
- Test results and analysis
- Regulatory submission documents
- Change control records

### 3.3 Reporting Requirements
The Developer shall provide regular progress reports to the Sponsor, including:
- Status updates on Development Work
- Risk assessments and mitigation plans
- Regulatory compliance status
- Any issues or delays encountered
- Recommendations for improvements

### 3.4 Post-Quantum Cryptography
For cryptographic development:
- Must comply with NIST PQC standards
- Must implement appropriate security controls
- Must maintain cryptographic documentation
- Must conduct security assessments

## 4. SPONSOR OBLIGATIONS

### 4.1 Access and Information
The Sponsor shall provide the Developer with:
- Access to necessary facilities and equipment
- Relevant technical specifications and requirements
- Existing documentation and data
- Contact information for key personnel

### 4.2 Regulatory Support
The Sponsor shall:
- Provide guidance on regulatory strategy
- Assist with FDA communications
- Maintain ultimate responsibility for regulatory submissions
- Ensure compliance with nonprofit requirements

### 4.3 Personal Goodwill Protection
The Sponsor shall:
- Maintain clear documentation of Personal Goodwill
- Ensure development work preserves asset protection
- Protect founder's rights under Georgia law
- Comply with bankruptcy and family law requirements

## 5. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

### 5.1 Ownership of Pre-existing IP
Each party retains ownership of its pre-existing intellectual property. The Developer grants the Sponsor a license to use any pre-existing IP incorporated into the Development Work.

### 5.2 Ownership of Developed IP
All intellectual property developed by the Developer under this Agreement shall be owned by the Sponsor, except for:
- Pre-existing IP of the Developer
- Personal Goodwill, which remains with the Sponsor's founder(s)
- Tools and methodologies developed independently by the Developer

### 5.3 License Grants
The Developer grants the Sponsor a perpetual, worldwide, royalty-free license to use any pre-existing IP incorporated into the Development Work.

### 5.4 Personal Goodwill Rights
The Developer acknowledges that:
- Personal Goodwill is protected under Georgia law
- Personal Goodwill cannot be transferred or assigned
- Personal Goodwill is protected from creditor claims
- The Sponsor's use of Personal Goodwill does not create rights in the Developer

## 6. COMPENSATION AND PAYMENT

### 6.1 Compensation
The Sponsor shall pay the Developer for the Development Work as specified in **Exhibit B** attached hereto.

### 6.2 Payment Terms
Payments shall be made according to the schedule specified in **Exhibit B**, subject to:
- Receipt of acceptable progress reports
- Verification of completed milestones
- Compliance with quality and regulatory requirements

### 6.3 Expenses
The Developer shall be responsible for its own expenses unless otherwise agreed in writing by the Sponsor.

### 6.4 Audit Rights
The Sponsor shall have the right to audit the Developer's records related to compensation and expenses under this Agreement.

## 7. CONFIDENTIALITY

### 7.1 Confidential Information
Each party agrees to maintain the confidentiality of the other party's confidential information and not to disclose it to third parties without prior written consent.

### 7.2 Medical Device Information
Confidential information includes but is not limited to:
- Technical specifications and designs
- Regulatory submissions and correspondence
- Clinical data and patient information
- Business plans and strategies
- Personal Goodwill documentation

### 7.3 Duration
Confidentiality obligations shall survive for [NUMBER] years after termination of this Agreement, except for trade secrets which shall remain protected indefinitely.

## 8. LIABILITY AND INDEMNIFICATION

### 8.1 Disclaimer of Warranties
THE DEVELOPMENT WORK IS PROVIDED "AS IS" WITHOUT WARRANTY OF ANY KIND, EXPRESS OR IMPLIED, INCLUDING BUT NOT LIMITED TO WARRANTIES OF MERCHANTABILITY, FITNESS FOR A PARTICULAR PURPOSE, AND NONINFRINGEMENT.

### 8.2 Limitation of Liability
In no event shall either party be liable for any indirect, incidental, special, or consequential damages arising from this Agreement.

### 8.3 Developer Indemnity
The Developer shall indemnify and hold harmless the Sponsor from any claims, damages, or expenses arising from:
- Breach of Developer's representations and warranties
- Negligence or willful misconduct of the Developer
- Violations of Regulatory Requirements caused by the Developer
- Infringement claims related to Developer's pre-existing IP

### 8.4 Sponsor Indemnity
The Sponsor shall indemnify and hold harmless the Developer from any claims, damages, or expenses arising from:
- Breach of Sponsor's representations and warranties
- Use of the developed medical device by the Sponsor
- Regulatory violations caused by the Sponsor

### 8.5 Medical Device Liability
The parties agree that:
- The Sponsor assumes ultimate responsibility for the medical device
- The Developer is responsible for development work quality
- Both parties shall cooperate in defending any claims
- Appropriate insurance coverage shall be maintained

## 9. REGULATORY COMPLIANCE

### 9.1 Quality System Requirements
The Developer shall implement and maintain a quality management system that includes:
- Design and development planning
- Design input and output requirements
- Design review and verification procedures
- Design validation and transfer processes
- Document and record controls
- Supplier and contractor controls
- Nonconforming product controls
- Corrective and preventive actions

### 9.2 Risk Management
The Developer shall conduct comprehensive risk management activities including:
- Hazard identification and risk analysis
- Risk evaluation and prioritization
- Risk control measures implementation
- Residual risk assessment
- Risk management file maintenance
- Post-market surveillance planning

### 9.3 Regulatory Submissions
The Developer shall assist the Sponsor with regulatory submissions including:
- 510(k) premarket notifications (if applicable)
- Design history file preparation
- Technical documentation compilation
- Clinical evaluation reports
- Risk management files
- Usability engineering files

### 9.4 Post-Market Requirements
The Developer shall assist with post-market activities including:
- Complaint handling procedures
- Adverse event reporting
- Field safety corrective actions
- Post-market clinical follow-up
- Software updates and patches

## 10. TERM AND TERMINATION

### 10.1 Term
This Agreement shall commence on the effective date and continue until completion of the Development Work, unless earlier terminated as provided herein.

### 10.2 Termination for Cause
Either party may terminate this Agreement for material breach by providing written notice. The breaching party shall have [NUMBER] days to cure the breach.

### 10.3 Termination for Convenience
The Sponsor may terminate this Agreement for convenience upon [NUMBER] days written notice to the Developer.

### 10.4 Effects of Termination
Upon termination:
- The Developer shall deliver all work in progress to the Sponsor
- The Sponsor shall pay for all completed and accepted work
- Confidentiality obligations shall survive
- Intellectual property rights shall be determined as provided in Section 5

## 11. GOVERNING LAW AND DISPUTE RESOLUTION

### 11.1 Governing Law
This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Georgia.

### 11.2 Dispute Resolution
Any disputes arising from this Agreement shall be resolved through:
- Good faith negotiation between the parties
- Mediation if negotiation fails
- Litigation in Georgia courts if mediation fails

### 11.3 Federal Preemption
To the extent that federal law (including FDA regulations) preempts state law, federal law shall control.

## 12. MISCELLANEOUS

### 12.1 Entire Agreement
This Agreement constitutes the entire understanding between the parties regarding the subject matter hereof.

### 12.2 Amendments
Any amendments must be in writing and signed by both parties.

### 12.3 Assignment
Neither party may assign this Agreement without the other party's prior written consent.

### 12.4 Personal Goodwill Assignment
Personal Goodwill may not be assigned, transferred, or licensed without explicit written consent from the Sponsor's founder(s).

### 12.5 Notices
All notices shall be in writing and delivered to the addresses specified below.

### 12.6 Severability
If any provision is found invalid, the remaining provisions shall remain in effect.

### 12.7 Waiver
