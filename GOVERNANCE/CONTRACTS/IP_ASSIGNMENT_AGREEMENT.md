# INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY ASSIGNMENT AGREEMENT

**This Agreement is made effective as of:** [DATE]  
**Between:** [ASSIGNOR NAME], an individual ("Assignor")  
**And:** P31 Labs, Inc., a Georgia nonprofit corporation ("Corporation")

## RECITALS

WHEREAS, the Assignor has developed certain intellectual property related to assistive communication technologies and medical devices; and

WHEREAS, the Corporation desires to acquire certain rights to such intellectual property for its nonprofit mission; and

WHEREAS, the parties wish to define the terms of such assignment while preserving certain protections for the Assignor;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants contained herein, the parties agree as follows:

## 1. DEFINITIONS

### 1.1 "Assigned Intellectual Property" means all right, title, and interest in and to the following, whether registered or unregistered:
- Patents, patent applications, and patent disclosures
- Copyrights and copyrightable works
- Trade secrets and confidential information
- Trademarks and service marks
- Know-how, processes, and techniques
- Software, source code, and object code
- Design rights and industrial designs
- Any and all other intellectual property rights

### 1.2 "Personal Goodwill" means the non-transferable intellectual property and technological developments that are inextricably bound to the identity of the Assignor and qualify as separate property under Georgia law (Miller v. Miller precedent).

### 1.3 "Licensed Technology" means certain intellectual property that is licensed to the Corporation for use but not assigned, as specified in Section 3.

## 2. ASSIGNMENT OF INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

### 2.1 Assignment
The Assignor hereby irrevocably assigns, transfers, and conveys to the Corporation all right, title, and interest in and to the Assigned Intellectual Property throughout the world.

### 2.2 Exclusions from Assignment
The following intellectual property is expressly excluded from this assignment:
- **Personal Goodwill:** All Personal Goodwill remains the exclusive property of the Assignor
- **Pre-existing Technology:** Intellectual property developed prior to [DATE] and not related to the Corporation's activities
- **Separate Inventions:** Intellectual property developed entirely independent of the Corporation and without use of its resources

### 2.3 Future Developments
The Assignor agrees to assign to the Corporation any intellectual property developed during their relationship with the Corporation that relates to the Corporation's mission or uses the Corporation's resources.

## 3. LICENSE GRANTS

### 3.1 License to Licensed Technology
The Assignor hereby grants to the Corporation a non-exclusive, royalty-free, worldwide license to use, reproduce, modify, distribute, and sublicense the Licensed Technology for nonprofit purposes.

### 3.2 Personal Goodwill License
The Assignor grants to the Corporation a limited license to use Personal Goodwill solely for the purpose of continuing the development and distribution of assistive technologies, subject to the following restrictions:
- License terminates upon dissolution of the Corporation
- License does not include rights to assign or transfer Personal Goodwill
- License is subject to Georgia family law protections
- License preserves the Assignor's rights under bankruptcy law

### 3.3 License Limitations
The licenses granted in this Section 3 do not include the right to:
- Assign or transfer the underlying intellectual property
- Use the intellectual property for commercial purposes
- Modify the intellectual property in ways that could jeopardize asset protection
- License the intellectual property to third parties without Assignor's consent

## 4. REPRESENTATIONS AND WARRANTIES

### 4.1 Assignor Representations
The Assignor represents and warrants that:
- They are the sole and exclusive owner of the Assigned Intellectual Property
- They have the full right and authority to make this assignment
- The Assigned Intellectual Property does not infringe any third-party rights
- There are no pending or threatened claims against the Assigned Intellectual Property
- The assignment does not violate any agreements with third parties

### 4.2 Personal Goodwill Representations
The Assignor represents that:
- Personal Goodwill qualifies as separate property under Georgia law
- Personal Goodwill is non-transferable and inseparable from the Assignor's identity
- Personal Goodwill is protected from division in bankruptcy proceedings
- The limited license granted does not compromise asset protection strategies

### 4.3 Corporation Representations
The Corporation represents and warrants that:
- It is a validly existing Georgia nonprofit corporation
- It has the authority to enter into this Agreement
- It will use the Assigned Intellectual Property for nonprofit purposes
- It will comply with all applicable laws and regulations

## 5. MEDICAL DEVICE COMPLIANCE

### 5.1 Regulatory Compliance
The parties acknowledge that certain Assigned Intellectual Property may be subject to FDA regulation as medical device technology and agree to:
- Comply with 21 CFR Part 820 quality system requirements
- Maintain appropriate documentation for regulatory purposes
- Cooperate with FDA inspections and investigations
- Report any potential regulatory violations

### 5.2 Quality Management
The Corporation shall:
- Implement and maintain a quality management system
- Follow design control procedures for medical device development
- Conduct risk assessments per ISO 14971
- Maintain design history files and technical documentation

### 5.3 Post-Quantum Cryptography
For cryptographic intellectual property:
- Must comply with NIST PQC standards
- Must maintain highest security standards
- Must be documented for regulatory review
- Must not introduce vulnerabilities

## 6. CONSIDERATION

### 6.1 Assignment Consideration
In consideration for the assignment of the Assigned Intellectual Property, the Corporation shall provide:
- [SPECIFY CONSIDERATION, e.g., $1.00 and other valuable consideration]

### 6.2 License Consideration
The licenses granted in Section 3 are provided without additional consideration.

### 6.3 Personal Goodwill Consideration
The limited license to Personal Goodwill is granted in consideration of the Corporation's commitment to:
- Preserve the Assignor's asset protection rights
- Maintain compliance with Georgia family law
- Protect the technology from creditor claims
- Continue the mission of assistive technology development

## 7. INDEMNIFICATION

### 7.1 Assignor Indemnity
The Assignor shall indemnify and hold harmless the Corporation from any claims, damages, or expenses arising from:
- Breach of Assignor's representations and warranties
- Infringement claims related to the Assigned Intellectual Property
- Violations of third-party rights

### 7.2 Corporation Indemnity
The Corporation shall indemnify and hold harmless the Assignor from any claims, damages, or expenses arising from:
- The Corporation's use of the Assigned Intellectual Property
- Breach of this Agreement by the Corporation
- Regulatory violations caused by the Corporation

### 7.3 Medical Device Liability
The parties agree that:
- The Corporation assumes responsibility for regulatory compliance
- The Assignor may be liable for willful misconduct or gross negligence
- Both parties shall cooperate in defending any claims
- Insurance coverage shall be maintained as appropriate

## 8. RECORDING AND NOTIFICATION

### 8.1 Assignment Recording
The Corporation shall record this assignment with appropriate government agencies, including:
- United States Patent and Trademark Office (USPTO)
- United States Copyright Office
- Any other relevant intellectual property offices

### 8.2 Third-Party Notifications
The Corporation shall notify any relevant third parties of this assignment, including:
- Licensees of the Assigned Intellectual Property
- Regulatory agencies (if applicable)
- Business partners and collaborators

### 8.3 Personal Goodwill Documentation
The Corporation shall maintain documentation of the Personal Goodwill protections, including:
- Copies of this Agreement
- Legal opinions regarding asset protection
- Records of compliance with Georgia law

## 9. GOVERNING LAW AND DISPUTE RESOLUTION

### 9.1 Governing Law
This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Georgia.

### 9.2 Dispute Resolution
Any disputes arising from this Agreement shall be resolved through:
- Good faith negotiation between the parties
- Mediation if negotiation fails
- Litigation in Georgia courts if mediation fails

### 9.3 Federal Preemption
To the extent that federal law (including FDA regulations) preempts state law, federal law shall control.

## 10. MISCELLANEOUS

### 10.1 Entire Agreement
This Agreement constitutes the entire understanding between the parties regarding the subject matter hereof.

### 10.2 Amendments
Any amendments must be in writing and signed by both parties.

### 10.3 Assignment
The Corporation may not assign this Agreement without the Assignor's prior written consent.

### 10.4 Personal Goodwill Protection
The Assignor's rights to Personal Goodwill may not be assigned, transferred, or licensed without the Assignor's explicit written consent.

### 10.5 Notices
All notices shall be in writing and delivered to the addresses specified below.

### 10.6 Severability
If any provision is found invalid, the remaining provisions shall remain in effect.

### 10.7 Waiver
Failure to enforce any provision shall not constitute a waiver.

## 11. SPECIFIC PROVISIONS FOR ASSET PROTECTION

### 11.1 Bankruptcy Protection
The parties acknowledge that:
- Personal Goodwill is protected from creditor claims in bankruptcy
- The limited license preserves asset protection strategies
- The Corporation's use does not create transferable rights
- Bankruptcy proceedings cannot affect Personal Goodwill rights

### 11.2 Divorce Protection
The parties acknowledge that:
- Personal Goodwill qualifies as separate property under Georgia law
- The limited license does not create marital property interests
- Asset protection strategies are preserved
- The Assignor's rights are protected in family law proceedings

### 11.3 Creditor Protection
The parties agree that:
- Creditors cannot reach Personal Goodwill through the Corporation
- The limited license does not create attachment rights
- Asset protection is maintained through proper structuring
- Legal opinions shall be maintained regarding protections

## 12. TERM AND TERMINATION

### 12.1 Term
This Agreement shall remain in effect for the duration of the applicable intellectual property rights.

### 12.2 Termination
Either party may terminate this Agreement for material breach by providing written notice. Termination does not affect rights in intellectual property already assigned.

### 12.3 Survival
Sections 4 (Representations), 7 (Indemnification), 11 (Asset Protection), and 12 (Miscellaneous) shall survive termination.
