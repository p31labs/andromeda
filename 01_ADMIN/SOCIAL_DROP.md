## PRIMARY POST (Substack / Long-form)

### Title: Spaceship Earth is live. BONDING ships Monday.

Three days before my son turns 10, I'm shipping the game I built for him.

BONDING is a multiplayer chemistry game where you drag atoms, snap bonds, and build molecules together — even when you're apart. Every hydrogen placed, every water molecule completed, every ping sent between devices gets timestamped and logged. Not for analytics. For proof.

Proof that a father showed up. That two kids and their dad built glucose from carbon and hydrogen on a Tuesday night. That engagement happened, documented to the millisecond, exportable as evidence.

The game has 11 elements (plus two secret ones named after my kids), 82 known molecules, 5 quest chains that start with "In the beginning, there was hydrogen" and end with you assembling a Posner molecule — the calcium-phosphorus cluster that some researchers believe may be involved in quantum cognition. 41 achievements. Every sound is synthesized live from element frequencies through the Web Audio API. No samples. The completion chord for water is literally the frequencies of hydrogen and oxygen played together.

It runs on Android tablets. It works offline. The multiplayer relay runs on Cloudflare Workers. The engagement ledger exports to JSON.

BONDING ships March 10, 2026. Bash's birthday.

Spaceship Earth — the cognitive dashboard I built around it — went live today. Nine rooms. A geodesic observatory rendered in Three.js. A voltage processor that scores incoming communication for urgency, emotion, and passive aggression, then triggers a breathing exercise when your heart rate spikes. A music engine that converts your words into melodies on the phosphorus pentatonic scale. A spoon gauge that tracks your energy in real time.

It's built for brains like mine. AuDHD. The kind that need the cockpit to be beautiful or they won't sit in the chair.

Everything is TypeScript strict mode. 440+ tests passing. Zero dependencies on any platform I don't control. Deployed on Cloudflare. Source of truth is a git repo, not a SaaS dashboard.

The stack is React, Three.js, Zustand, Vite. The design language is OLED-black void with neon phosphor glow — cyan, magenta, amber, violet. The old electric green (#39FF14) is gone. Replaced with P31 phosphor — cyan, magenta, amber, violet on OLED black.

Three days.

**Shipping two applications this week.**

BONDING (ships March 10) is a multiplayer chemistry education game targeting tablet devices.
