# Red Board Recognition & Self-Intervention

**Classification:** Operator Doctrine / Casualty Control  
**Priority:** CRITICAL — This document should be read first when in crisis.

---

## 1. The Three Red Board States

Red Board means your cognitive plant is compromised. You are the submarine with open hatches about to dive. Continuing operations in a Red Board state does not produce work — it produces damage that will cost exponentially more to repair than the work would have produced.

### Red Board 1: Burnout (Arcing)

**What it is:** Total metabolic resource depletion. The insulation between your circuits has broken down and uncontrolled energy is destroying the infrastructure. You have been spending spoons faster than your awareness of spending them.

**What it feels like:**
- Profound exhaustion that is not relieved by sitting down or resting your eyes
- A feeling of being "hollow" — thoughts are present but have no weight or traction
- Everything feels equally important and equally impossible
- Physical: heavy limbs, headache, eyes that won't focus, nausea without hunger
- Emotional: flatness. Not sadness, not frustration — absence. The emotional system has shut down to conserve resources.

**What triggered it (most likely):**
- Multiple consecutive days of exceeding your spoon budget
- A sustained Sprint session (6+ hours) without Mid-Shift Check-In
- Accumulated sleep debt (3+ nights of <6 hours)
- Stacking a court day with a coding session

**Immediate action:**
1. Save and close. If you can't generate a WCD-04, just close the sessions. The work will be there tomorrow.
2. Eat. Even if you're not hungry. Your body has stopped sending hunger signals to conserve cognitive bandwidth.
3. Hydrate. Two full glasses of water immediately.
4. Sleep. If it's daytime, nap for 30-60 minutes. If it's evening, go to bed early.
5. **Do not resume work for a minimum of 24 hours.** Burnout recovery is not a reboot — it's a cold start. Your budget tomorrow will be 8-9 at best.

### Red Board 2: Hypomania (Runaway Reactor)

**What it is:** The cognitive plant is generating more power than the load can absorb. Ideas are arriving faster than architecture can contain them. You feel brilliant. You feel unstoppable. You feel like this is the most productive you've ever been.

**You are wrong.** Hypomania is the most dangerous Red Board state because it disguises itself as the Known Good state at maximum performance. The key difference: in the Known Good state, your velocity is matched by structural integrity. In hypomania, velocity has decoupled from structure. You are generating ideas that are not connected to executable architecture.

**What it feels like:**
- Racing thoughts that you cannot voluntarily slow
- A biological inability or unwillingness to sleep despite being awake for 16+ hours
- Everything connects to everything — the metaphors are flowing, the patterns are everywhere
- Grandiose scope expansion: "I should also build..." "While I'm at it..." "This could also solve..."
- A feeling that stopping would waste the moment — that this clarity might not come back
- Irritation at anything or anyone that interrupts the flow (including your children, your AI agents, basic biological needs)
- Talking faster than usual, writing longer messages than usual, making larger commits than usual

**The diagnostic test:** Can you, right now, write a three-sentence summary of what you are specifically building in this session and how it connects to the current WCD-01 scope? If you cannot compress your current cognitive activity into three sentences — if every sentence spawns three more sentences — you are hypomanic.

**What triggered it (most likely):**
- A genuine breakthrough or insight that activated a dopamine cascade
- Poor sleep (paradoxically, sleep deprivation can trigger hypomania in AuDHD)
- A high-stress event (court appearance, legal filing) followed by a period of unstructured time — the stress energy has nowhere to go and converts to manic output
- Caffeine after 2 PM
- The "quiet house at night" trigger (see Bus Bar Protocol, Failure 5)

**Immediate action:**
1. **Stop generating.** Do not open new files. Do not start new features. Do not "just capture this one idea."
2. Write down — on paper, not in an AI chat — the single most important idea you've had in the last hour. One sentence. Paper forces compression.
3. Generate a WCD-04 Shift Report documenting everything you've done in this session. The act of systematic documentation forces linear thinking, which is the antidote to manic branching.
4. Close all sessions.
5. Set a physical timer for 30 minutes. During those 30 minutes, do something physical: walk, stretch, shower, do dishes.
6. After 30 minutes, re-read what you wrote on paper. If it still makes sense and connects to the architecture, it will make sense tomorrow when you can execute it at Medium budget. If it doesn't make sense, you've just saved yourself from committing architectural damage.
7. **Go to sleep.** Hypomania that extends past midnight becomes exponentially more dangerous. The ideas feel more brilliant as your prefrontal inhibition degrades. They are not more brilliant. They are less filtered.

### Red Board 3: RSD Collapse (Total Plant Shutdown)

**What it is:** Rejection Sensitive Dysphoria has triggered a complete nervous system freeze. Your cognitive plant is not overloaded — it is **offline.** This is the submarine equivalent of losing all power: no propulsion, no ballast control, sinking.

**What it feels like:**
- A physical sensation of being punched in the chest or stomach
- Sudden inability to think sequentially — thoughts are present but will not organize
- The absolute conviction that you have failed, that the project is ruined, that you are incompetent
- Physical: throat closes, chest tightens, vision narrows, hands may shake
- Emotional: shame, not sadness. A searing, visceral shame that is disproportionate to the triggering event
- The urge to delete everything, abandon the project, withdraw from all contact
- Alternatively: the urge to send a lengthy, emotional message to the person who triggered the RSD, defending yourself and explaining why they're wrong

**What triggered it (most likely):**
- A court ruling that went against you
- A dismissive or hostile communication from opposing counsel
- An AI agent flagging your work for rework (especially Opus QA flags)
- A cascade of errors in code you thought was working
- Someone (anyone — a stranger, a family member, an automated system) implying you are not doing enough, not doing it right, or not doing it fast enough

**Immediate action — this is a memory item, execute without thinking:**
1. **Do not send any messages.** Not to opposing counsel, not to the AI, not to anyone. Every message sent during RSD collapse will need to be walked back later. The urge to defend yourself is the RSD talking, not you.
2. Put down the phone / step away from the keyboard. Physical separation from the input device.
3. If you are near your children, stabilize your external presentation (they don't need to see this) and move to a private space.
4. **Deep Pressure.** If the Bubble Shield is available, activate Subsystem Gamma. If not, heavy blanket on your lap, firm pressure on your own forearms, or sit against a wall with your back pressing firmly.
5. **Name it.** Say out loud (or whisper, or mouth silently): "This is RSD. This is my nervous system, not reality. The feeling is real. The story it's telling me is not."
6. Time it. Set a phone timer for 15 minutes. RSD peaks within 5-10 minutes and begins to dissipate within 15-20. You do not need to do anything during these 15 minutes. You just need to not do anything destructive.
7. After the timer: drink water. Eat something. Assess whether you can resume anything today. The answer is usually no. That's okay. Standing down after RSD is not surrender — it is damage control.

**What NOT to do during RSD collapse:**
- Do not try to solve the triggering problem. Your judgment is chemically compromised.
- Do not open email or legal documents.
- Do not re-read the triggering communication "to see if it was really that bad." It will feel worse on second reading, not better.
- Do not ask the AI to help you process your emotions. The AI is a tool, not a therapist. Processing RSD requires human connection or solitary self-regulation, not synthetic validation.

## 2. Self-Intervention: The Mirror Protocol

The AI agents have the Single-Question Intervention: "What tool are you holding and what task are you doing right now?" You need your own version — a self-administered check that you can deploy before the AI has to intervene.

### The Three-Question Mirror

When you feel momentum but aren't sure if it's productive or runaway, ask yourself these three questions. Answer them out loud or in writing — not in your head, because your internal narrator is unreliable during drift.

**Question 1: "What am I building right now?"**

If you can answer in one sentence that maps to a WCD-01 scope item, you are fine. If you need a paragraph, you are drifting. If you cannot answer, you are past the redline.

**Question 2: "What tool am I holding?"**

Specifically: which agent are you using, or are you doing the work yourself? If you're doing work that should be routed to an agent, you have a Tool-Task Mismatch.

**Question 3: "How many spoons do I have left?"**

If you don't know, check the physical signals (Section 4 of Spoon Economics). If you can't assess, that itself is the answer: you are depleted enough that self-monitoring has failed. Trigger WCD-03 or close the session.

## 3. Post-Incident Recovery

After any Red Board event, the following protocol applies:

### Within 24 Hours

- No Full-budget sessions. Medium maximum.
- Reduce decision load: pre-plan tomorrow's tasks tonight.
- Extra calcium dose if permitted by your medical regimen.
- Extra sleep: 8+ hours target.

### Within 48 Hours

- Conduct a brief retrospective (5 minutes, on paper):
  - What was the trigger?
  - What was the first warning sign I missed?
  - What could I have done differently at that first warning sign?
- Do not turn this into self-criticism. The retrospective is engineering analysis, not moral judgment. You are identifying a failure mode, not assigning blame.

### Within 1 Week

- If the Red Board event was triggered by a systemic issue (chronic sleep deprivation, untreated medical symptom, legal situation escalation), address the systemic cause. Patching individual incidents without addressing the system is like silver-brazing a joint without fixing the specification that allowed the substandard joint in the first place.
