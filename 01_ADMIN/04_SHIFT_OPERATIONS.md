# Daily Shift Operations & Routines

**Classification:** Operator Doctrine / Operational Rhythm

---

## 1. The Shift Model

Your work operates on a **shift model**, not a continuous-availability model. You are not "always on." You go on shift, you execute work within a defined scope and budget, and you go off shift. This is borrowed directly from submarine watchstanding: the reactor doesn't care how important you think your analysis is — when your relief arrives, you turn over the watch and leave.

For you, "your relief" is the WCD-04 Shift Report. When you generate the report, you have turned over the watch. The work exists in a documented state that your future self or an AI agent can pick up without loss of continuity. Walking away from documented work is not abandoning it — it is proper shift turnover.

## 2. Pre-Shift Procedure (15-20 minutes)

### Step 1: Biological Baseline (5 minutes)

Before touching any device, run the Pre-Shift Checklist from Operator Baseline §6:

```
[ ] Calcium taken
[ ] Vitamin D taken
[ ] Eaten within last 4 hours
[ ] Hydrated within last 2 hours
[ ] Sleep > 6 hours last night
[ ] Physical pain assessment: ___ / 10
[ ] Court/legal event today: Y / N
[ ] Honest spoon estimate: ___ / 12
```

If your net available spoons after baseline costs is 0 or negative: **Stand Down.** No shift today. This is not a suggestion. This is a procedural requirement.

### Step 2: Environmental Setup (3 minutes)

- **Workspace:** Desk clear of non-essential items. One screen maximum for AI work. Phone face-down or in another room unless needed for 2FA.
- **Sensory check:** Is the room temperature comfortable? Is there distracting noise? If environmental conditions are hostile to your sensory profile, address them now. You will not address them once hyperfocus engages — you will simply drain spoons compensating for them unconsciously.
- **Timer visible:** Physical timer or large clock positioned where you can see it without context-switching. You will not track time internally. Accept this and externalize it.
- **Water within arm's reach.** If you have to get up to get water, you won't.

### Step 3: Review Previous Shift State (5 minutes)

Open the most recent WCD-04 (Shift Report). Read:
- What was completed last shift
- What was not completed and why
- What the Next Shift Instructions say
- Whether any REC is open

If a REC is open, you are operating within a maintenance boundary. Re-read the WCD-05 to confirm what subsystems are isolated and what tests are required before restoration.

### Step 4: Draft WCD-01 (5 minutes)

Based on the WCD-04 review and your current spoon budget, draft the Pre-Job Brief for this shift:

- **Scope:** One primary deliverable. Not two. Not "and also." One.
- **Files in Play:** List every file the agent may touch. If you can't list them, your scope is too broad.
- **Budget Tier:** Set based on Step 1 assessment. Do not upgrade mid-shift. You may downgrade.
- **Time Limit:** Set a hard end time. Write it on paper. Stick it to the monitor. When the time arrives, the shift ends regardless of completion state.

### Step 5: Open the Session

Now — and only now — open the AI chat. Paste the WCD-01. Begin work.

## 3. During Shift: Operational Discipline

### The 60-Minute / 10-Exchange Rule

If your session reaches either 60 minutes of elapsed time or 10 conversational exchanges without producing a verifiable deliverable (committed code, completed document, rendered UI), a WCD-03 Mid-Shift Check-In is mandatory. This can be self-initiated or triggered by the AI agent.

The WCD-03 forces you to answer:
- Am I still working on the WCD-01 scope? (Scope drift check)
- Is the tool in my hand appropriate for the task? (Tool-Task Mismatch check)
- How many spoons do I estimate I have left? (Spoon trajectory)
- Should I continue, downgrade budget, or end the shift?

### The Single-Thread Rule

Work on one task at a time within a session. The AuDHD brain generates branching ideas continuously. Each branch feels urgent. Each branch is a spoon expenditure. Your job during a shift is not to explore every branch — it is to complete the single deliverable defined in the WCD-01 and log the branches for future shifts.

**Practical implementation:** When a tangential idea appears mid-session, write it down in a single line (a scratchpad file, a sticky note, the WCD-04's "Next Shift Instructions" field). Do not open a new tab. Do not ask the AI about it. Do not "just quickly look into it." Capture the idea in one sentence and return to the primary thread. The idea will still be there when you have a fresh WCD-01 dedicated to it.

### Hydration and Movement Triggers

Set two alarms:
- **Every 45 minutes:** Drink water. Stand up for 60 seconds. This is not optional and not flexible. Hyperfocus will make you forget. The alarm is the override.
- **Every 90 minutes:** Walk away from the desk for 5 full minutes. Not to the kitchen for coffee (that's a stimulant, not a break). Away. Outside if possible. Let your visual system defocus.

These interruptions feel disruptive. They are not. A 60-second standing break costs 0 spoons. Sitting motionless for 3 hours costs 1-2 spoons in accumulated physical tension and postural strain that you will not notice until the shift ends and your back seizes up.

### When to End the Shift Early

End the shift immediately — do not wait for a "good stopping point" — if any of the following occur:

- Your hands are cold and you didn't notice until now
- You've re-read the same AI output three times without comprehending it
- You're irritated at the AI and composing a response that starts with "No, what I meant was..."
- Your child needs you. Full stop. Close the session. The WCD-04 takes 2 minutes. Your child's need is happening now.
- Physical symptoms: tingling, cramping, visual disturbance, chest tightness
- You've been in the session for longer than the time limit you set in the WCD-01

## 4. Post-Shift Procedure (10 minutes)

### Step 1: Generate WCD-04 (5 minutes)

Before closing any session, request or self-generate the Shift Report:
- What got done (file paths, commit hashes if applicable)
- What didn't get done and why
- Architectural decisions made and their rationale
- REC status (open, closed, unchanged)
- Next Shift Instructions: explicit, specific, actionable

**The rule:** If you cannot generate a WCD-04, the session has exceeded your capacity. Generate an abbreviated version (3 bullet points: done, not done, next step) and end. A partial WCD-04 is infinitely better than no WCD-04.

### Step 2: Physical Transition (3 minutes)

- Close all tabs. All of them. Not "minimize" — close.
- Stand up. Stretch. Walk to another room.
- Change your physical state in some observable way: change clothes, wash your hands, step outside. This creates a **somatic boundary marker** that signals to your nervous system that the shift is over. Without this physical transition, your brain will continue processing the shift for hours, consuming spoons during what you think is "rest time."

### Step 3: Debrief (2 minutes)

Ask yourself three questions (answer on paper or mentally):
1. Did I stay within the WCD-01 scope? (If no: what pulled me off?)
2. Did I stay within my spoon budget? (Check physical signals now)
3. Is there anything that needs immediate attention before the next shift? (If yes, write it down and step away. "Needs attention" does not mean "needs attention right now.")

## 5. Weekly Rhythm

### Monday: Planning

- Review previous week's WCD-04 reports
- Identify the top 3 deliverables for the week
- Check calendar for court dates, medical appointments, school events
- Allocate spoon budgets to available work days
- Draft the week's first WCD-01

### Tuesday–Thursday: Execution

- Standard shift operations as described above
- Maximum 2 Full-budget sessions per week
- Maximum 1 session per day (with rare exceptions for Medium-budget follow-up sessions separated by 2+ hour breaks)

### Friday: Administration

- Documentation catch-up (WCD-04s that were abbreviated, WCD-05s that need routing)
- Gemini session for narrative/documentation if needed
- Legal document review (scheduled, not reactive — do not open legal emails on execution days if avoidable)
- Light work only: Medium or Low budget

### Weekend: Recovery

- No scheduled P31 work. If inspiration strikes, capture it in one sentence on paper. Do not open an AI session.
- Focus on parenting, household maintenance, and genuine rest
- One exception: brief WCD-02 task cards (Low budget, <30 minutes) for time-sensitive items, maximum once per weekend

## 6. The Non-Negotiable Rules

These rules exist because each one was learned through a failure. They are not aspirational. They are structural safety requirements.

1. **No new WCD-01 after 10 PM.** Your calcium is at its nadir. Your judgment is degraded. What feels like inspiration is likely hypomania.

2. **No P31 work on court days.** The spoons are already spoken for. There are no surplus spoons to allocate.

3. **Maximum one Full-budget session per day.** Two Full sessions in a single day is borrowing from tomorrow at 1.5x interest.

4. **The children come first.** If a parenting need arises mid-shift, the shift ends. The WCD-04 takes 2 minutes. Guilt about interrupted work is cheaper to process than guilt about an unmet parenting need.

5. **If you can't generate a WCD-01, you're not ready to start a session.** The inability to scope the work is itself a signal that your prefrontal cortex isn't online enough to execute the work.

6. **Sleep is not negotiable.** Below 6 hours: automatic Red Board. Your creative output at 2 AM is not worth the 2-day recovery cost.
