## 2. Corporate Governance and Separateness

Maintaining strict corporate separateness is essential for any nonprofit. Under Georgia law, courts may disregard the corporate form under the "alter ego" doctrine only where (1) the entity was used as a mere instrumentality for personal affairs with such unity of interest that the separate personalities no longer exist, and (2) the corporate form was used as a subterfuge to promote fraud or injustice.

### P31 Labs maintains separateness through:

**Financial separation:** All organizational revenue flows through the dedicated P31 Labs Stripe account (`acct_1T6z3U4Kt3K4WuBD`). No personal expenses are paid from organizational accounts. No organizational funds are deposited into personal accounts.

**Governance documentation:** The organization operates under a formal Work Control Document (WCD) system with 88+ tracked work units, a canonical Ground Truth document, and structured project management.

**Board of Directors:** Joseph Tyler Cisco (independent director), Brenda O'Dell (director).

**Operational records:** GitHub version control (github.com/p31labs, 7 public repositories), CI/CD pipelines, automated testing (659+ tests across the ecosystem), and Genesis Block telemetry with server-side SHA-256 hashing provide objective, timestamped evidence of organizational activity independent of any individual.

## 3. Intellectual Property Ownership and Licensing

All software developed under P31 Labs' mission is owned by the organization and released under open-source licenses consistent with the charitable purpose. BONDING is licensed under AGPL-3.0. Core infrastructure licensing will be determined upon 501(c)(3) approval.

### 3.1 Patent Eligibility Under Ex parte Desjardins (2025)

On November 4, 2025, the USPTO designated **Ex parte Desjardins** (Appeal No. 2024-000567) as precedential, significantly expanding patent eligibility for AI and software innovations under 35 U.S.C. § 101. Under this framework, AI algorithms that provide concrete improvements to computer technology — such as The Buffer's communication processing and Fawn Guard pattern detection — are eligible for patent protection. P31 Labs preserves its filing rights through the 12-month grace period under 35 U.S.C. § 102(b)(1)(A), with GitHub commits and Zenodo publications establishing prior art dates.

### 3.2 Slander of Title Protection for Software IP

Under Georgia law, the tort of slander of title (O.C.G.A. § 51-9-11) extends to intangible intellectual property including computer software. *Compris Technologies, Inc. v. Techwerks, Inc.*, 274 Ga. App. 673 (2005). Any party making false communications casting doubt on P31 Labs' exclusive ownership of its software — for example, asserting personal marital claims over organizational assets in communications to grantors or partners — would be subject to this cause of action.

## 4. Assistive Technology Classification

P31 Labs' products serve a documented medical and accessibility need. The hardware (Node One, Node Zero) and software (The Buffer, Fawn Guard, BONDING) are developed as assistive communication technologies consistent with the classification at 21 CFR § 890.3710 (Powered Communication System). These are not consumer entertainment products; they are cognitive prosthetics developed by and for neurodivergent individuals.

- **The Buffer** is a non-device clinical decision support tool exempt from FDA premarket review under the 21st Century Cures Act.
- **Node One** is classified as FDA Class II exempt under 21 CFR § 890.3710.

This classification is relevant to ADA Title II proceedings: the development tools and hardware at the founder's workspace are necessary accommodations for a documented disability, not discretionary personal property.
