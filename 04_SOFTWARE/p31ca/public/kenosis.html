02. The Self-Healing Mesh

"Kenosis" is the theological concept of self-emptying — the voluntary relinquishment of power or privilege. Applied to network architecture, it describes a system that assumes operational load without requiring a central authority to hold it together.

The mesh does not have a "brain." It has distributed consensus. Any node can route to any other node. The loss of one node does not degrade the informational completeness of the remaining set — it reorganises around the gap.

VULNERABILITY REMOVED
Wye neutral collapse — the single point of failure in hub-spoke that allows institutional capture or technical failure to bring down the entire system.
PROPERTY GAINED
Byzantine fault tolerance — up to ⌊(n−1)/3⌋ nodes can fail or act maliciously without disrupting consensus across the remaining nodes.

The social dispatch pipeline — content generation, posting, and telemetry reporting — runs fully autonomously through the mesh. The operator does not need to be online or aware for the system to maintain its social presence and reporting obligations.
