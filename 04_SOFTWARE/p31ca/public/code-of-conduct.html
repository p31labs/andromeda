Code of Conduct

Version 1.0.0 · Effective 2026-05-02 · Adapted from Contributor Covenant 2.1 (CC BY 4.0) with P31-specific clauses.

Our pledge

We pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

P31-specific clauses

In addition to the standard Contributor Covenant text, the following clauses apply in P31 spaces:

  • Voice doctrine adherence. The DELTA language and Public Voice standards are enforced. Marketing words on the avoid-list and naval/military metaphors are doctrine, not preference.
  • No medical advice on public surfaces. P31 is sub-medical-grade by design. No clinical recommendations, dosing instructions, or substitutes for professional care.
  • K₄ family mesh confidentiality. Children's full names are protected by initials policy (S.J., W.J.). Mesh state is private.
  • Operator-condition awareness. The operator's hypoparathyroidism creates real-time cognitive variability. Calcium-deficit days are a medical state; "Spoon deficit" notices are a hard pause.
  • No surveillance of children. Personas that interact with children must not log content, profile children, or be used as a parental surveillance tool.
  • No engagement-maximization patterns. Streaks, FOMO hooks, infinite scroll, and notification-cadence tuning are off-doctrine.
  • No cloud LLM in operator-confidential surfaces. Routing counsel, triage, or phos persona prompts through any cloud LLM endpoint is a hard ban.

Reporting

Email conduct@p31labs.org (forwards to the operator until a Code of Conduct committee is constituted). Until the email domain is live, open a private security advisory on the relevant repository — the operator triages both. Acknowledgment within 72 hours.

Enforcement ladder

  1. Correction — private written warning
  2. Warning — consequences for continued behavior; no interaction during cool-off period
  3. Temporary ban — no public or private interaction for a specified period
  4. Permanent ban — for sustained patterns of violation

Anonymized enforcement actions are summarized annually in the Transparency Report. Counts only — no names.
