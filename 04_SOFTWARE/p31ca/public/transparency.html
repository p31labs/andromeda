Warrant canary

As of 2026-05-02, P31 Labs has never received a National Security Letter, FISA order, or any government request for user data that we are prohibited from disclosing.

This sentence will appear in every future transparency report, updated to that report's publication date, unless and until it is removed. Removal of this canary is itself a signal.

Why publish in Year One when every answer is “zero”?

Most organizations wait until their first government request, takedown, or breach. We publish in Year One so the cadence is established, the floor is visible, and any future delta is measurable. Funders need to see the practice, not just the promise.

The Signal Foundation pattern is the right one: annual, plain language, no PR spin, no asterisks.

What we could provide if compelled

If P31 ever receives valid legal process, we would respond, push back through counsel, notify the affected user unless prohibited, and disclose in the next report to the maximum extent the law permits.

Architecturally, the data we hold is bounded:

  • Cognitive Passport content: none. The passport lives in the user's browser. We could not produce it because we do not have it.
  • K₄ mesh state: per-user Durable Object scope. Producible under valid process; we would notify and push back.
  • Hub access logs: Cloudflare Pages retains per its own policy. We do not separately retain.
  • Donor identity: Stripe holds; we hold amount, date, and email used at checkout for receipts and 990 reporting.

Operator-condition disclosure

P31 is a one-operator nonprofit founded by a person with hypoparathyroidism (ICD-10 E20.9) and AuDHD (autism + ADHD, late-diagnosed 2025). This affects our reporting cadence (annual; emergency operator-state events may delay days, never weeks), reporting voice (direct, no submarine metaphors, CI-enforced), and reporting honesty (when something cannot be done, the report says so). Disclosed so funders, auditors, and family members reading this report understand the operating constraint.
