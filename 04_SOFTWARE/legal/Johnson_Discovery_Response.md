## PRELIMINARY STATEMENT

Defendant is a forty-year-old individual who was diagnosed in 2025 with Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) and Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD). Additionally, Defendant has suffered from Chronic Hypoparathyroidism (ICD-10 E20.9) since 2003, a rare endocrine disorder affecting calcium regulation and presenting ongoing risk of seizure, cardiac arrhythmia, and cognitive impairment.

Defendant is proceeding pro se following the withdrawal of prior counsel, J. Alan Welch, on February 5, 2026. Defendant currently receives SNAP and Medicaid benefits and has zero current income from employment.

Defendant objects to several of the Plaintiff's requests on the grounds of overbreadth, undue burden, proportionality, privilege, and relevance. These objections are made in good faith and are necessary due to Defendant's documented cognitive processing deficits, which impose a genuine burden on his ability to respond to expansive discovery while managing his medical conditions and navigating these proceedings without the assistance of counsel.

---

## GENERAL OBJECTIONS

The following General Objections are incorporated by reference into each specific response provided herein:

**1. Overbreadth and Proportionality.** Defendant objects to each request to the extent it is overbroad, unduly burdensome, or not proportional to the needs of this case as defined by O.C.G.A. § 9-11-26(b)(1). The Plaintiff's requests seek to impose an excessive informational burden on a pro se litigant with documented cognitive disabilities.

**2. ADA Accommodation.** Defendant has documented disabilities (ASD, ADHD, and Chronic Hypoparathyroidism) and has pending ADA accommodation requests with this Court. Defendant's medical condition involves non-linear metabolic instability resulting from calcium dysregulation, which impacts his capacity for high-volume administrative tasks.

**3. Privilege and Work Product.** Defendant objects to requests seeking attorney-client privileged material, work product, or research prepared in anticipation of litigation. This specifically includes AI-assisted litigation research, which serves as a cognitive prosthetic for the Defendant and is protected under O.C.G.A. § 9-11-26(b)(3).

**4. Trade Secrets.** Defendant objects to requests seeking trade secrets or confidential commercial information protected under O.C.G.A. § 9-11-26(c)(7) and the Georgia Trade Secrets Act, O.C.G.A. § 10-1-760 et seq.

**5. Separate Property.** Defendant objects to requests seeking information regarding post-separation intellectual property, nonprofit work product, and assistive technology development that constitute non-divisible separate property and are irrelevant to the equitable division of marital assets.
