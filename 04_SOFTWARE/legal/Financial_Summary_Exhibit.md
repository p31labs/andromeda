## KEY ANALYTICAL SUBSTRATES

### Inflow Misalignment

While total deposits in November 2025 reached $82,541.60, this was driven by a single TSP hardship withdrawal of $49,396.60 (net of 20% withholding). This influx was not income but a liquidation of the primary marital retirement asset necessitated by a medical crisis and the withdrawal of legal counsel.

### The Welch-East Attrition

Between September and October 2025, the Defendant paid approximately $12,878.69 in attorney fees to Joe East and J. Alan Welch. Both attorneys withdrew shortly after receiving these payments, forcing the Defendant into a pro se posture while his liquid assets were reaching critical exhaustion levels.

### Current Capacity Baseline

As of the close of the March 2026 statement period, the EveryDay (1360), Joint (5344), and Active Duty (1352) checking accounts all maintain a balance of $0.00. The only remaining asset is the **$5.00 minimum** in the Membership Savings (9978) account.

### Velocity of Depletion

Within approximately **90 days** of the TSP deposit, the Defendant's liquid capacity crashed from **$8,128.66** to a negative balance of **-$836.97** in January 2026. This demonstrates that the Defendant did not "dissipate" assets in the traditional sense of marital waste, but rather engaged in a desperate "bill sprint" to maintain housing and legal representation after the loss of his DFAS salary.

### Medical Exigency Impacts

Recurring expenditures for medications and behavioral health for the Defendant and the minor children remained constant throughout the collapse timeline, despite the total cessation of the DFAS salary after October 22, 2025.
